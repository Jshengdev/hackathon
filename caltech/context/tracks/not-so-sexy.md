# Track: "Not So Sexy"

## Official
- **Prize:** Whoop sports tracker / watch (~$300 retail)
- **Description:** "Skip the hype…let's talk about the stuff that might not be so flashy. This track is for hackers who find beauty in logistics, insurance technology, database migration, waste management, and other niches that the standard hacker may not think of. Bring us some solutions that solve trillion-dollar problems, but may not be so flashy on instagram. We need stuff that keeps the world running and can make a fortune while doing it."

## What They ACTUALLY Want (read between lines)
- **Trillion-dollar TAM in unsexy verticals.** Examples called out: **logistics, insurance tech, database migration, waste management** — all real industries that VCs/founders fawn over privately.
- "Make a fortune while doing it" — they want **commercial viability framing**, not just charity/social-good. This is a B2B / vertical-SaaS / infra-tools track in disguise.
- "Stuff that keeps the world running" — infrastructure, plumbing, the boring middleware of civilization.
- The phrasing "the standard hacker may not think of" implies judges expect **deep domain insight** — show you understand the actual workflow being disrupted (claims processing, freight routing, data warehouse migration, hauler scheduling, etc.).

## How to Win
- **Pick a real, named vertical** — don't say "I built an AI for industries." Say "I built an AI underwriter for commercial property insurance carriers."
- Show you've done **homework** on the domain — buyer persona, real workflow, pain quantification (cost of bad decisions, hours wasted on a task)
- Quantify the prize: "$X market, $Y wasted annually on this task today"
- Demo on **realistic data**, not toy data
- **Actual revenue model** stated in the pitch — who pays, how much, why now

## Competition Level
- **LOW-MEDIUM** — "not sexy" is intentionally repellent to most hackers. Less competition. The teams that DO show up here are typically high-conviction founders. Quality > quantity in this pool.
- **High EV** for a team that wants to differentiate.

## Track Stacking
- **Not So Sexy + YC** — pick a pre-2022 YC company in logistics/insurance/infra and AI-native it
- **Not So Sexy + Palohouse** — perfect overlap; Palohouse wants real founders, this track wants real businesses
- **Not So Sexy + Best AI** — AI-applied-to-boring-thing is the thesis VCs love right now
- **Not So Sexy + IFM K2** — reasoning model applied to insurance underwriting, freight planning, supply-chain optimization, db migration planning, etc.
