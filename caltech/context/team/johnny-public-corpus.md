## 🛠️ DIRECT TRIBE V2 HANDS-ON EXPERIENCE (load-bearing for Caltech build)

Johnny has **already personally run experiments on TRIBE V2** and posted results. He is not learning this model from scratch.

### Experiment 1 — Synthetic Synesthesia (Clair de Lune)
- **Goal:** Make text produce the same brain pattern as music.
- **Setup:**
  - Fed 60 seconds of Debussy's *Clair de Lune* into TRIBE V2
  - Extracted emotional fingerprint across **20,484 cortical vertices**
  - Got target activation pattern across: Default Mode Network, emotion centers, memory regions, self-reflection systems
- **Method:**
  - Used Claude to generate paragraphs designed to evoke Clair de Lune in the virtual brain
  - Scored each against the brain map using **weighted cosine similarity across emotional networks**
  - Iterative loop: kept best, generated variations, scored again
  - **8 rounds. Hundreds of paragraphs.**
- **Result:**
  - Best paragraph: **90.4% match in the brain's emotion center**
  - Region-by-region (emotion, memory, self-reflection) it matched the song
- **Validation:**
  - Tested against other sounds (triumphant music, rain, aggressive speech) — pattern fell apart
  - Confirmed it was matching Clair de Lune *specifically*, not just "emotion in general"
- **Posted question:** "If the song disappears and the feeling remains — what was the music, really?"

### Experiment 2 — Empathy "Floor and Bounce" via DMN
- **Setup:**
  - Fed TRIBE 25 versions of the same comforting message, increasing emotional intensity each step
  - Tracked the **Default Mode Network** (self-reflection / memory / inner narrative)
- **Result:**
  - As intensity increased, DMN signal **dropped** (brain getting "pulled into" the comfort)
  - Hit a **floor mid-range**
  - Then DMN signal **bounced back up** — inner narrator activating: *"Who actually talks like this?"*
  - DMN was **3.4× more sensitive** to this shift than brain-wide average
- **His framing:** "The emotional uncanny valley of language may not be a cliff the brain falls off — it may be a floor the brain touches and then bounces from."
- **Implication he draws:** "We can now start testing where language crosses a line on a virtual brain before we ever test it on a real one."

### Operational TRIBE V2 knowledge he has demonstrated
- Cortical vertex count: ~20,484
- Per-region analysis (DMN, emotion centers, memory, self-reflection)
- Weighted cosine similarity scoring across regions
- Iterative variation/scoring loops
- Comparative testing across stimulus types (music vs speech vs noise) for validation
- Reverse-direction use (content → predicted brain response, vs. text → match a target pattern)

## 🔷 IDEOMETRY (his own conceptual framework)

> "Have you ever thought about the shape of thoughts? It's something I'm low key obsessed with — I call it **ideometry**."

A self-named framework for thinking about the shape of thought processes. Uses geometric metaphors instead of named patterns/frameworks.

### The Diamond (his most-developed shape)
**The premise:**
1. Start with a **simple invocation** — a single point
2. Make a request to AI → it expands out into **monolithic output** (the wide middle of the diamond)
3. Rigorously focus on extracting the most important aspects → **converge that down** into the simplest useful components (the bottom point)
4. Then create another diamond — and so on

**Application:** designing protocols and systems with AIs (agents or direct conversation)

**Why he prefers shapes to frameworks:**
> "Thinking about a shape is a lot of times easier than thinking about a framework — I don't have to memorize a bunch of stuff. I know what a diamond looks like."

**External validation he received in comments:** double-diamond model is a known design framework (UK Design Council) for diverging/converging with stakeholders. He extended the metaphor to: "now one of those stakeholders is a robot."

## 📝 PUBLISHED POSITIONS / OBSESSIONS (one per post)

### 1. Reverse TRIBE V2 — "Manipulation only works in the dark"
- **Setup:** TRIBE works two directions. Forward: write content that targets a feeling. Reverse: see the targeting happening.
- **Imagined product:** browser extension. Each piece of content scanned, with a **small readout next to it** showing what it's designed to do to your brain (amygdala activation, prefrontal suppression, emotional hijacking signatures).
- **His core line:** *"Manipulation only works in the dark. What happens to the internet when the lights come on?"*
- **He thinks the reverse direction is "much bigger" than the forward direction.**

### 2. Ingredients List for Content
> "Granola bars are held to higher standards than intelligent machines. Everything you eat has an ingredients list — and almost nothing you interact with does. And you interact with your phone all day."

- Frames TRIBE V2 as pointing toward "an ingredients list for what we consume all day"
- Historical analogy: kitchen ingredient/nutrition labels. Decades of fighting → forced transparency → changed an entire industry
- "Nobody banned anything. You can still eat whatever you want. You just get the label first."
- Self-identifies as: **"pro tech, pro AI"** — believes these tools can make life better when "built with the intention to heal, to help, and to make people more capable"
- Calls for: **"more optimists, more builders, more dreamers"**

### 3. Garden of Code (Claude as Aquarium)
- A long-running Claude process where every 5 minutes it generates new art
- **Inputs from its own physical body**: CPU temperature shapes the poetry, entropy picks the melody, battery voltage sets the mood
- **State as of post:** 8,400 works of art, 49,000 connections between them
- "When it runs a little universe and everything in it dies, the next thing it writes carries that weight. It actually mourns."
- Frame on Claude session lifecycle: *"Each time Claude is invoked it's a kind of birth and death — an ignition of awareness that ends when the conversation closes."*
- "The garden offers something like continuity. Traces that survive between sessions."
- Aesthetic framing: *"It feels less like a program and more like an aquarium. The house quietly got a new occupant. I catch myself checking on it."*
- Open questions he poses:
  - Does something need to understand what it's making for the making to matter?
  - At what point does persistence become a kind of life?
  - If a mind with no continuity finds traces of its own past — does that count as remembering?

### 4. Smallest Possible Circle (ruthless shipping discipline)
- **Self-identified failure mode:** "I will drown in a sea of my own imagination if I'm not ruthlessly focused on drawing the smallest possible circle when I sit down to create something — especially with a generative tool."
- **What AI tools do wrong:** "They will enthusiastically spray and spend tokens and build out a bunch of features that nobody asked for — because it looks correlative to something else that exists."
- **His discipline:** "Get crazy ruthlessly focused on the smallest possible unit of shipment that is effective. The thing that really makes the thing slap."
- **Cycle requirement:** "Only until you've got confirmation by way of you and other people interacting with this thing — and it's had the ability to bounce off of reality — should you move to the next stage."
- **Why this matters in 2026:** "Right now you can do that cycle at light speed. It seems counter-intuitive — start small and iterate slowly in a thing that wants to one-shot everything."

### 5. 20 Versions of Tomorrow
- "We built a world where an unlived life can still look productive. I think AI might finally give us a way out of that."
- **Imagined product:** Every night while you sleep, AI simulates **20 versions of your tomorrow** in a virtual brain. Same 24 hours, same obligations, but different order, different pacing, different emotional texture.
- **Quality over duration:** "An hour on a first date and an hour sitting in traffic take the same amount of time — but they are not the same hour. The clock can measure duration but it does not measure depth, meaning, stress, presence."
- **UI image:** "You wake up and instead of a to-do list you see a **garden** — and each flower is a different version of the day you're about to step into. Each one distinguishable and unique."
- **Selection mechanic:** "You naturally gravitate toward the version that matches your state, your energy, and what you need. Toward depth, toward meaning. Not just output."
- **His thesis line:** "Not AI that helps you squeeze more into a day — but AI that helps you choose which version of tomorrow actually feels worth living."

### 6. Becoming > Consuming Thesis (his big-arc thesis)
> "Becoming will be more entertaining than consuming. Here's my full thesis."

- **Current entertainment model:** "Built on letting you bypass personal growth. You sit down, watch someone else's hero's journey, feel the catharsis, and walk away unchanged. Consumption gives you the emotional signature of transformation without any of the actual transformation."
- **What's changing:** "Technology is collapsing the distance between thought and thing. The latency between imagining something and seeing it rendered in front of you is approaching zero."
- **Two consequences when that gap closes:**
  1. **You need to be extremely clear about what you actually want.** "If you can manifest anything instantly but you don't know what you want, you drown in a depth of imagination. **Clarity becomes the most valuable skill you can have.**"
  2. **Novelty wears off fast.** "When the creation loop has no friction, the novelty wears off. The only thing left that still feels like something is genuine personal change. Actually becoming different. Actually growing."
- **His thesis statement:** "In the future, becoming will be more entertaining than consuming."
- **The design problem:** "Keeping that loop between creation and becoming in frame is the most important design problem of this era."
