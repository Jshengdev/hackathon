## ⚠️ READING ORDER FOR FUTURE EYES

This document is the canonical narrative spine for the Caltech HackTech 2026 pitch deck. Three things govern everything below:

1. **The judges have already watched the demo video before they see the deck.** The deck's job is **gap-fill** — origin, philosophy, granular how-it-works, deliberate choices, vertical futures.
2. **Concept priming is non-negotiable.** Every concept is earned through an AI-CAN / AI-CANNOT inevitability triad (§B). Plain speech is policy (§C).
3. **Every slide is a tweet.** Hook-or-die. One idea per slide. Conversational, not corporate. (§O — writing discipline policy adapted from the X-posts skill.)

## §B — THE CONCEPT-PRIMING SEQUENCE (the inevitability chain)

This is the rhetorical engine that runs underneath every beat. **Each concept enters through what AI can already do, then what it can't, then the only honest next move.** When played in order, every step feels like the audience discovered it themselves — *of course we did that, what else could we possibly have done?*

| Link | AI **CAN** | AI **CANNOT** | The only honest next move |
|---|---|---|---|
| **L1 — The seen world** | describe what's in front of a camera | tell whether the person in frame was afraid, exhausted, focused, or rotting | *describing is not understanding* |
| **L2 — The action-data trap** | measure what humans did (time, count, speed) | see *what they were carrying* while they did it | *systems built on action-data treat humans like machines* |
| **L3 — The body's own answer** | *(neuroscience can show what someone felt — fMRI scanners watch the brain)* | scale (you'd need a scanner per human per minute) | *until something predicts the brain without the scanner, the inside stays invisible* |
| **L4 — TRIBE V2 unlock** | *(Meta just shipped a brain encoder — feed it any video/sound/text, out comes ~20K cortical points)* | *(no asymmetry — this is the unlock. Hold the silence here.)* | *for the first time, every video has a brain attached to it* |
| **L5 — Signal to sense** | give 20,000 numbers per second | make any manager read 20,000 numbers | *one specialist agent per main brain system — a swarm of seven* |
| **L6 — Parts to a paragraph** | give seven partial readings | leave a manager holding fragments | *a moderator listens to all seven plus the camera, writes one paragraph* |
| **L7 — The trust test** | write a paragraph that could be hallucinated | *(the AI hallucination problem)* | *don't trust — falsify. Iterate eight rounds. Project back to brain-space. Geometric grounding.* |
| **L8 — Naming what we built** | see what humans **did** | see what humans **felt** | *biological signal as operational data — the empathy layer* |

**Key truth:** L4 is the *only* link without an asymmetry — that's the awe beat. Everywhere else, the form is `CAN / CANNOT / so we…`. At L4 the form breaks open: *here is the thing that exists, that we did not have to build, that we get to use.* Don't rush it. The audience needs a beat to absorb that the brain encoder is real, free, and on HuggingFace.

## §C — JARGON TRANSLATION TABLE (plain-speech policy)

The first time a real name (TRIBE V2, K2, Opus 4.7) appears, it is announced once and then renamed in plain speech for the rest of the pitch. Names exist for sponsor credit and falsifiability; **plain speech exists so the audience keeps following.**

| Never say | Always say |
|---|---|
| "VLM" / "vision-language model" | "the AI behind your phone camera that names what it sees" |
| "TRIBE V2" *(after first naming)* | "the brain encoder" |
| "fsaverage5 / 20,000 cortical vertices" | "twenty thousand points across the brain" |
| "Yeo7 networks" | "the seven main systems your brain runs on" |
| "K2 specialist swarm" | "a swarm of seven AI specialists, one per brain system" |
| "K2 moderator" | "the moderator agent that writes the paragraph" |
| "K2-as-evaluator iterative loop" | "the same seven, now grading the paragraph it wrote" |
| "embedding-proxy falsification" | "we project the paragraph back into brain-space and check the match" |

#### BEAT 5 — Because of that… *(Falsification — geometric grounding + Clair de Lune chip, honest version)*

**Spoken spine (~40s):**
> An AI-written paragraph could still be made up. So we don't trust it. We **falsify** it.
>
> The same seven specialists grade the paragraph against what their region said. Eight rounds of rewriting. Then we project the final paragraph back into brain-space and check the match.
>
> Either the paragraph lives in the same cortical neighborhood as the original brain pattern, or it doesn't. **Cosine distance doesn't lie.**
>
> We proved this loop architecture last year on Clair de Lune music: 90.4% match against the target brain pattern. Refused to match aggressive speech. Refused to match rain. *For this demo we swapped the per-round scorer — the original used the brain encoder forward, ours uses the K2 evaluator swarm — and swapped the falsification metric — the original used the encoder forward, ours uses sentence-transformer cosine projection. Same loop. Different, demo-reliable instruments.*

## §O — X-DISCIPLINE WRITING POLICY (every slide is a tweet)

Adapted from the X-posts skill. The "users" are the judges scrolling under attention pressure. Every slide is judged by tweet rules:

### Hook or die
- Slide 1 of every section is a hook. If it doesn't stop the scroll, the rest doesn't matter.
- The room's first one second on a new slide decides whether they read it or wait for the next.

### One idea per slide
- A slide with two ideas is a slide with no ideas.
- If a beat has three things to say, it's three slides.

### Brevity is power
- Slide copy ≤ 12 words. Headlines ≤ 7 words.
- One number per slide. Earn it.

### Conversational, not corporate
- Verbs > nouns. Active voice. Cut adverbs.
- "We built" beats "Our solution provides." Use the human voice.
- Zero corporate speak. Zero "synergy", zero "leverage", zero "robust solution."

### Each slide stands alone
- A judge who joined late on slide 9 should still be able to understand slide 9.
- The deck is a thread. Each tweet stands alone but builds the thread.

### Open loops between slides
- The end of a slide should make you want the next one.
- "Different data." → next slide → "Cortical signal as operational data." That's a cliffhanger and a payoff.

### What to avoid
- ❌ Two-column comparison slides
- ❌ Bulleted lists of more than 3 items
- ❌ Acronyms before they're defined in plain speech
- ❌ Slideument density (slide that doubles as document)
- ❌ Process diagrams without an emotional payoff slide adjacent
- ❌ Logo walls
- ❌ "Thank you" slides

### Quick checklist for every slide
- [ ] Does the line stop the scroll?
- [ ] One idea?
- [ ] Under 12 words?
- [ ] Stands alone?
- [ ] Sets up the next slide?
- [ ] Zero corporate speak?
- [ ] One number max?
