# Rescorla-Wagner / Berlyne on Surprise

**Source:** Rescorla-Wagner model of classical conditioning (1972); Berlyne's novelty/surprise distinction. Review: PMC3858647 — https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3858647/

**One-line claim:** Surprise is the felt response to *expectation violation* — the gap between a prediction the brain made and the outcome it observed. *"Organisms only learn when events violate their expectations."*

**Why we cited it:** Underpins the §3.1 "toggle" mechanic in our demo-theatre canonical. Our locked Land beat — *"watch the algorithm's pick vs. your actual taste in the same brain space"* — is a toggle that engineers expectation violation: the judge predicts the patterns will look similar (both are "your" brain), but they're measurably different.

**Effect on memory (Rescorla-Wagner literature):** *"Expectation violation before or during learning has been shown to trigger an adaptive encoding mechanism, resulting in better memory for unexpected events."* The judge will *remember* a surprise three weeks from now; they will not remember an unsurprising explanation.

**For our arc:** the toggle MUST set up an implicit prediction in the first 30 seconds. Candidate prediction: "this person's brain on their feed and on something they'd actually choose are about the same — they're both 'them.'" Then the toggle breaks it: they're not the same. The pattern shift IS the surprise.

**Engineering implication (anti-pattern):** if the judge sees the "after" state before the toggle happens, surprise is killed. The compare frame must be the toggle's *output*, not its setup. Hide the comparison until the toggle fires.
