# 005 — "Best Use of AI" as the hard target

## Decision

The "Best Use of AI" track is the hard target. Our architecture, demo, and pitch all answer the question: *what does proper AI use look like in 2026?* We are not pitching ethics — we are pitching impact + competitive positioning.

## Date locked

2026-04-25 (Saturday morning hypothesis-frame articulation).

## Locked by

Johnny (verbatim, theme README live thread):
> "Base it off of the AI slop thing. Hard target the best use of AI — and the best use of AI is fighting back against the things where AI can be used terribly and irresponsibly. Not out of ethics, not out of being good people — out of sheer impact. Augmented human intelligence should be the way humans actually interact with AI in a sense that it actually creates human ingenuity, spawns the human renaissance, and challenges a lot of previous beliefs and things that aren't really good about society. Each sponsor should be intrigued and in love with what we have." [source: `research/wiki/themes/ai-paradox-invisible-use-cases/README.md` line 111]

## Why

- **Best Use of AI is universal** — every project at HackTech 2026 is graded on the AI rubric in Round 1 regardless of which other track it's in. So the AI rubric is the only competition floor that's literally inescapable. Better to hard-target it than ride it incidentally [source: `caltech/context/tracks/best-use-of-ai.md` lines 8–13].
- **The AI-paradox theme** (Hak/comprehension-debt + Filter-World + trends-slop) gives the team a defensible thesis that *isn't* "another AI agent" — it's "AI that fixes what AI is breaking."
- **Impact-first framing** echoes IFM judges' explicit signal: "they will pick the project with the larger market impact, even over a project with a deeper / cleverer tech stack" [source: `caltech/context/sponsors/ifm-k2.md` lines 102–106].
- The hypothesis frame is **not an ethics pitch** — that's a deliberate frame to avoid coming across as preachy / political [source: `research/wiki/themes/ai-paradox-invisible-use-cases/window-2-research-deepening/000-coordination-and-thesis-snapshot.md` line 29].

## What this opens

- Architecture must un-black-box AI (auditor diff visible; citation chips clickable; swarm reasoning streams) — that becomes the AI-rubric "wow."
- Sponsor stacking under one architecture: K2 = audit-grade reasoning, Listen Labs = voice/human-layer, Ironside = spatial visibility, all read as *facets* of "proper AI use."
- "Augmented human intelligence" framing becomes the connective tissue across sponsor pitches.

## What this closes

- AI-as-replacement framings (anything where the system *replaces* a human in the loop) — those would contradict the hypothesis frame.
- Dark-pattern UX (constant validation / sycophancy / personalized tone) per Johnny's anti-patterns [source: `caltech/context/team/johnny.md` line 53].
- Pure-novelty demos with no impact thesis — the IFM judges will filter them out.

## Reversibility

Reversible only if a sponsor talk Saturday surfaces a fundamentally different judging criterion the team hadn't accounted for. Currently no signal of that.

## Open tension (per protocol — not resolved)

**T1 from `500-elicitation-qa-pass.md`**: source 003 names *"augmentation"* as one of the four corpus-mean answers AI gives by default to strategic questions ("trends slop"). Picking "augmented human intelligence" as the hypothesis framing means picking the trends-slop word as the un-trends-slop framing. This contradiction is **held, not resolved** — the team owns whether to lean into the word or rephrase.
