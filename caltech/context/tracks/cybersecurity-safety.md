# Track: Cybersecurity / Safety

## Official
- **Prize:** NuPhy Air 75 v3 keyboard (low cash value — keyboard is ~$130)
- **Description:** "AI Safety, traditional security, and more. As systems advance in capability and scale to unprecedented proportions, safety has become more important than ever. Develop algorithms, applications, and other software to secure cyberspace."

## What They ACTUALLY Want (read between lines)
- The framing leads with **AI Safety** — Caltech / MLH judges in 2026 are wired into alignment, jailbreak resistance, agent oversight, model misuse. Traditional infosec is mentioned but feels like a "yes also that" addendum.
- Phrase **"systems advance in capability and scale to unprecedented proportions"** = directly evoking frontier-model risk discourse. They want projects that engage with the AI-as-attack-surface OR AI-as-defender narrative.
- "Algorithms, applications, **and other software**" — open-ended, but biased toward shipped artifacts over papers/research.

## How to Win
- AI safety angle > generic infosec — judges this year will perk up at agent guardrails, prompt-injection defense, jailbreak detection, watermarking, content provenance, deepfake detection, model interpretability tools
- Need a **concrete threat model** stated upfront — judges in safety land respect specificity
- Live red-team / blue-team demo lands hard
- Tooling that other developers/companies could actually adopt = more credible than research toy

## Competition Level
- **Medium** — fewer pure-security hackers in the wild, but the AI Safety framing draws lots of "agent guardrails" projects that all blur together. Differentiation matters.
