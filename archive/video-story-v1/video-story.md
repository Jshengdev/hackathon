## §0. Overview

**Total runtime:** 180 seconds (3:00).
**Format:** Cinematic launch video for Caltech HackTech 2026 Round 2 pitch + Devpost embed.
**Aspect ratio:** 16:9 1080p minimum, 4K preferred. Vertical 9:16 cut also produced for social.
**Frame rate:** 24fps for Acts 1+4 (cinematic). 30fps for Act 3 (screen-recording integration).
**Voiceover register:** Acts 1+4 = *Black Mirror / Severance / Mr. Robot* — intimate, calm, slightly cinematic. Acts 2+3 = warmer-guide register (StoryBrand customer-as-hero). Final close (last 12s) = protagonist's voice, transformed.
**Color register:** Act 1 cool/desaturated → Act 2 neutral with directional warm light through "third door" → Act 3 hot-pink-yellow on cortical glow against neutral background → Act 4 warm-uplift (relief, not triumph).

**Emotional arc** (per `emotional-depth-demo-theatre-canonical.md` 8-emotion taxonomy):
- Act 1 lands **Recognition** (intimate defeat) → **Awe** (vastness of converging brains) → **Foreboding** (BCI extension same-trade-same-trap)
- Act 2 lands **Hope** (third door opens) → **Curiosity** (architecture preview)
- Act 3 lands the four BEAT-emotions in compressed form: **Recognition** + **Awe** (BEAT-1) → **Surprise** (BEAT-2 hover-bridges) → **Awe** + **Surprise** (BEAT-3 within-subject toggle) → **Pride** + **Comfort** (BEAT-4 Land card)
- Act 4 lands **Relief** + **Ownership** (protagonist transformed; the 17-year-old voice returns from desperation to control)

**Doctrines binding the shoot:**
- **No reverse inference.** Voiceover NEVER says "amygdala fired = user felt fear." Always observational: *"responded to attention-grabbing cues" / "observed measurable response."*
- **No clinical claims.** *"Content discovery, not clinical diagnosis."*
- **No inflated TRIBE numbers.** ~20,000 vertices on fsaverage5; ~25 trained subjects. NEVER 70K voxels / 700 subjects.
- **No sub-second predictions.** TRIBE is 1Hz with 5s HRF lag; respect this in animation pacing.
- **Within-subject contrast only.** *"Your brain on your feed vs. your brain on something else"* — never population-norm comparisons.
- **No "Spotify Wrapped of Your Brain"** as product name — format reference only.
- **Final wording polish deferred to Saturday 6 PM** ([TBD-FINAL-PASS] markers in voiceover blocks).

## §1. One-Page Treatment (the elevator)

A Gen-Z teenager — 17 years old, born on the algorithm, no "before" reference frame — opens the video defeated: *"I've tried to think for myself. But every time I do, the algorithm pulls me back. I've given up trying."* Twenty teenagers, identical posture, identical micro-second reactions to identical Reels content. The platforms that called themselves expressions of taste are recommendation algorithms wearing the costume of personalization. The genitive is a lie. Two doors appear: refuse and fall behind, or use it and become hollow. Then a third door materializes — light pouring through.

The product opens. We use Meta's brain-encoding model to read what content does to her brain. We use a swarm of region-specialist agents — running fast enough to be live only because of K2's ~1,300 tokens per second — to translate that signal into language. The brain lights up as she scrolls. Her amygdala responds to attention-grabbing cues. Her default-mode network — the part of her that's *her sense of self* — fires the same way for every scroll. The pattern repeats hauntingly. Then we run the same brain on a hand-curated short film. Same brain. Different input. Completely different pattern. The lock wasn't structural. It was learned.

Five Wrapped cards swipe through. The fifth card asks her to pick a brain region she didn't use. She picks Broca's Area — language production. The system shows her content **formats** that would activate it: long-form essays, poetry, language-learning, wordplay games. No links. No "go buy this." A voice lands: *"We don't want to recommend anything to you because that's how the algorithm works. We want to give you accessible information using AI so that you can make good judgments."*

She walks through the third door. Today it's Reels. In five years it's brain chips. Same trade. Same trap. We're building the design pattern for human-AI partnership before the cognitive interface becomes invisible. Last month, this same engine generated text producing 90.4% of Clair de Lune's brain pattern with no music. We know how this works. And we have an opinion about when AI should — and shouldn't — be used. The system you just watched is built entirely on the YEA side. Manipulation only works in the dark. She just turned the lights on.

## §2. ACT 1 — BLACK MIRROR SETUP (0:00 – 0:35)

**Total runtime: 35s.** Cinematic spine. Highest production value of the entire video. Slow-motion. Color cool/desaturated. Music: low strings, ambient hum, almost subaural. Sound-design reference: *Black Mirror* / *Severance* / *Mr. Robot*.

### Scene 1.1 — The opening defeat (0:00 – 0:08)

**Visual.**
- Slow-motion shot of a teenager (Maya, the persona — 17yo, casting brief in §7) scrolling Reels. Phone screen is the only light source. Face unreadable. Rim-lit by phone glow.
- Behind her, fragments of feed content blur and bleed into each other (use compositing in post — overlay 5-6 Reels frames at 8% opacity, drifting at different speeds).
- Hold on her face for 4 seconds. Phone glow flickers as feed scrolls.
- Cut to her hand on the phone screen — thumb pulling content up, mechanical, repetitive.

**Voiceover.** (Maya's voice — intimate, defeated. Recording register: close-mic, 3 inches; soft tone; SLIGHTLY hollow, like she's said this to herself many times. *Inciting incident lands at desperation, NOT curiosity* — this is the Sophia validation pass 2 rewrite.)

> **MAYA (VO):** *"I've tried to think for myself. But every time I do, the algorithm pulls me back. I've given up trying."*

**Sound design.** Ambient hum from 0:00. Low strings enter at 0:03 (single sustained note in the cello range, ppp dynamic). At 0:06, add a single soft electronic pulse synced to thumb-pull.

**Camera.** Dolly shot, slow push-in on Maya's face for the first 4 seconds, then static for the cut to thumb.

**Lighting.** Single key from phone screen direction (color temp ~6500K, blueish). Fill from a small bounce off-camera right (very low intensity, ~3200K, slight warmth). No backlight — keep the silhouette half-hidden.

### Scene 1.2 — The convergence reveal (0:08 – 0:17)

**Visual.**
- Hard cut. Same teenager. Then a second teenager in same posture. Then a third. Then a wide shot — twenty teenagers in a grid, different faces, different races, different settings, all scrolling, all the same posture.
- The twenty Reels frames sync up across all twenty screens — at 0:13, the same content beat (e.g., a TikTok dance drop, a true-crime jump-cut) plays simultaneously across all twenty.
- The synchronization is uncanny. Hold for 2 seconds on the synced beat.

**Voiceover.** (Maya's voice continues, slightly more present — she's noticed the pattern.)

> **MAYA (VO):** *"Instead, everyone is converging to the same point. Your Spotify is no longer your music taste — it's a recommendation algorithm. Your YouTube isn't yours. Your Reels — you can't select any of that."*

**Sound design.** At 0:13 (the synced content beat moment), a single sharp sound — a tape-stop or quick reverse — punctuates the synchronization. Strings sustain underneath.

**Camera.** First cuts (one teenager → two → three) are static medium shots, 1 second each. Wide shot of twenty teenagers held for 5 seconds with very slow push-in.

**Lighting.** Each teenager rim-lit by phone (same blue cast). Background varies (bedroom, classroom, bus stop, kitchen) but lighting is consistently cool.

**Casting note for Scene 1.2.** Twenty distinct teenagers — mix of races, genders, settings. Can be intercut from stock OR shoot 5-6 actual extras and composite into 20-grid (cheaper). All in scrolling-Reels posture. NO smiling. NO talking. Just scrolling.

### Scene 1.3 — The structural failure named (0:17 – 0:27)

**Visual.**
- Quick cuts (each ≤ 1 second):
  - Spotify logo morphing into a data-extraction icon (cable plug, pipe outflow, etc.)
  - YouTube logo morphing into surveillance-camera icon
  - Instagram/Reels logo morphing into hand-puppet icon

**Note on logos.** Use trademark-safe stylized versions or generic icons. DO NOT use actual Spotify/YouTube/Instagram logos in final cut to avoid trademark issues.

### Scene 1.4 — The trilemma + BCI extension + third-option tease (0:27 – 0:35)

**Visual.**

**Voiceover.** (Tone shifts — Maya's voice slightly uplifted, the tease.)

> **MAYA (VO, slight uplift):** *"You have two options. Don't use it — and fall behind. Or use it — and give up everything that makes you you. Today it's the algorithm. In five years it's brain chips. Same trade. Same trap. We came to build the third option."*

- Age 17-19 (looks ≤ 18). Gender flexible. Race flexible. Casting biased toward someone who reads as an "actual Gen-Z teen" — not a stylized actor.
- **Voice quality:** medium-low register, not breathy. Capable of intimate-defeated AND ownership registers. Capable of holding a long-line VO without breaks.
- **Look:** unaffected. Minimal makeup. Real bedroom-comfort wardrobe (oversized hoodie, t-shirt, etc.). NOT model-styled.

**Direction notes.**

**Scene 1.1 (defeated open).** Maya has said this to herself many times. It's not a fresh thought; it's an old wound. Read SOFTLY, not for drama. Almost as if the camera caught her saying it under her breath. SLIGHTLY hollow tone — not despair, just exhaustion.

**Scene 1.2 + 1.3 (convergence + structural failure).** Slightly more articulated. She's noticed something. The voice gains presence. By "the genitive is a lie" she's almost intellectualizing — distancing herself from the felt friction by naming it.

**Scene 1.4 (trilemma + tease).** Slight uplift — the recognition that there might be another option. NOT triumphant; just a flicker of possibility.

**Scene 4.2 (close, transformed).** The full transformation. Same person. Voice has CONTROL now. The "ownership" register. The "I just turned the lights on" line is delivered with quiet certainty — NOT victory-lap energy.

### Guide — Voiceover (the architecture/explainer voice)

**Casting brief.**
- Adult, gender flexible. Casting brief: *"sounds like the person who would explain Notion or Linear or Granola in a launch video — calm, present, in control, not breathless."*
- **Voice quality:** medium register, warm without being saccharine. Capable of clinical-observational (Acts 3.1, 3.4) AND wonder-inflected (Act 3.4 surprise) registers.
- **Reference:** Apple voiceover register, but slightly more present/personal. Or: Notion launch video voice. Or: a calmer Mr. Robot narrator.

**Direction notes.**

**Act 2 (introducing the product).** Calm. In control. Slightly warm. NOT salesy. *"You keep the choice"* should land as a statement of fact, not a slogan.

**Act 3 Scene 3.1 (observational hook).** Clinical. Observational. *"A measurable response"* — emphasis on "measurable." Strip any tone of "this is amazing." Just describe what's happening.

**Act 3 Scene 3.2 (specialist + cross-talk).** Confident, explanatory. Slight pride in the system architecture — not in self.

**Act 3 Scene 3.3 (pattern repetition).** Slow down slightly. Weight on "rhythm." The realization should be felt, not asserted.

**Act 3 Scene 3.4 (toggle surprise).** Wonder-inflected. *"The same regions, the same person — firing in a completely different pattern"* should land as a discovery, not a claim. Pause briefly between "completely different pattern" and "the lock the algorithm set wasn't structural."

**Act 3 Scene 3.5 (positioning moment).** This is the most important VO moment in the entire video. Read SLOWLY. *"We don't recommend anything to you"* lands first. Pause. *"That's how the algorithm works."* Pause. *"We give you accessible information so you can make your own judgment."* Each clause holds its own weight.

**Act 4 Scene 4.1 (credibility chip + rubric).** Confident, summative. Slightly slower than Act 3.5. *"Built entirely on the YEA side"* should land as a quiet flex, not a brag.
