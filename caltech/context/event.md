## Judging Mechanics

### Round 1 — Science Fair (10am–12pm Sun, ~2 hrs)
- Each team gets a number, judges get assigned ~5–6 projects within a single track
- Teams present **~5 min** to assigned judges
- Judges score using **track-specific rubric AND the AI rubric** (AI is scored on EVERY project)
- For Cyber/Creativity/"Not So Sexy": top 3 per group advance
- For Best AI Implementation: top 3 chosen straight from Round 1 scores

### Round 2 — Top 9 Showcase (1–1:30pm Sun, ~30 min)
- Top 3 per main track (9 teams) present **~3 min** to ALL judges + audience (hackers welcome)
- Judges rank 1st/2nd/3rd within each track
- Best AI is mostly locked in from Round 1 but judges can request additional presentations

### What this means for us
- **AI quality is universal** — every project is graded on AI rubric regardless of track
- Need TWO presentations: a 5-min depth pitch and a 3-min punchy showcase pitch
- Round 2 is where "wow factor" decides — not just feature completeness
- Sponsored challenges have separate judging tracks (don't show up here) — those are evaluated by sponsors directly
