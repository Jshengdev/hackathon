# Track: Creativity

## Official
- **Prize:** Logitech MX Master 4 mice (low cash value — ~$100/mouse × 4)
- **Description:** "Tech is one of the most powerful canvases. What will you paint? This track is for projects that push the boundaries of generative art, interactive storytelling, novel user experiences, or tools that can empower the next generation of creatives."

## What They ACTUALLY Want (read between lines)
- Two distinct sub-themes: **(a) artifact** (generative art, storytelling) and **(b) tool** (creative-empowerment software). The "OR tools that can empower" feels recently-added — judges want to reward both makers AND meta-makers.
- "Push the boundaries" — they want **novel UX or novel medium**, not "Midjourney but with a button"
- Lowest-cash prize on the board — likely to attract demo-y projects that *look* great but lack substance. Substance + visual wow = strong moat.

## How to Win
- **Visceral demo** — must look/feel different in the first 10 seconds
- Ideally an **interactive** experience the judge can touch in 5 min
- Original aesthetic (custom shaders, novel input modality, generative system) > stock model output
- A creator tool with one clear "moment of joy" beats a polymath tool

## Competition Level
- **Medium-high** — creativity track is a magnet for design-first hackers. To stand out you need either deep technical novelty (custom diffusion pipeline, real-time graphics) OR a wildly novel concept.

## Track Stacking
- **Creativity + Best AI** — generative art / agentic tools for creators
- **Creativity + Sideshift** — billion-dollar consumer creative product (think early Procreate, early Figma)
- **Creativity + Listen Labs** — narrative simulation, character-driven storytelling using social simulation
- **Creativity + Palohouse** — pitching as a creator-tools startup
