# Donald Miller — Building a StoryBrand

**Source:** Donald Miller, *Building a StoryBrand: Clarify Your Message So Customers Will Listen* (HarperCollins, 2017).

**One-line claim:** *"The Hero of your brand story is not you. The Hero is your Customer. You are the Guide."* The customer's problem must be specified at three layers: external (what's literally happening), internal (how it feels), philosophical (why it matters).

**Why we cited it:** Used in our demo-theatre canonical (§2.3 Recognition, §2.7 Pride, §3.5 cited testimony). The Land-beat reframe Johnny landed on — *"watch the algorithm's pick vs. your actual taste in the same brain space"* — is structurally StoryBrand-shaped: the Gen-Z teen is the hero, the system is the guide, the transformation is the user being more themselves.

**Three-layer problem-spec applied to our locked persona (Gen-Z AI-native):**

| Layer | Our specification |
|---|---|
| **External** | They scroll their feed. The recommender shapes what they see. |
| **Internal** | They can't tell which thoughts are theirs vs. converged from the feed. They feel a hollowness they can't name. |
| **Philosophical** | A generation never had the chance to develop autonomy at the cognitive level. The convergence of how a generation thinks IS the convergence of culture. |

**For our arc:** the press release's "user quote" must come from the *customer's* perspective (a 17-year-old's voice), not the founder's. The leader quote is the founder/Johnny voice. Two distinct voices.
