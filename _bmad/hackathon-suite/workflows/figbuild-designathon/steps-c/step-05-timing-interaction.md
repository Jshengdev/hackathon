### ROUND 5: THE CORE INTERACTION

### 5. Frame Round 5

"**Final round: The Core Interaction**

Not features. Not a feature list. The ONE thing that happens between human and tool. The single loop that IS this product."

### 6. Facilitate Yap

**Prompt A:** "Do you MAKE something, and the tool shows you where it sits in vector space?"

**Prompt B:** "Do you ask a QUESTION, get AI's average answer, then see your own answer's divergence from it?"

**Prompt C:** "Do you look at your HISTORY and see the shape of your thinking over time — your personal vector signature?"

**Prompt D:** "Is there a TRAINING element — the tool helps you practice going further out? Like exercising your divergence muscle?"

**Prompt E:** "What's the 'oh shit' moment the FIRST TIME someone uses it? Describe that scene."

**Prompt F:** "What's the feeling AFTER using it? Seen? Proud? Curious? Challenged? Understood?"

**Prompt G:** "Teri's frame: 'It's the humanizing version of what AI and human interaction looks like.' What does that mean in one interaction?"

### Divergent Seeds — Interaction

- "What if the loop is ADVERSARIAL? You try to trick the tool. You try to be divergent on purpose. And it shows you: 'that was forced divergence, not real.' The tool can tell the difference between genuine and performative originality."
- "What if the interaction is a DIALOGUE? You make a move, the tool responds with the average move, you see the gap, and then you choose: go further out or come back in?"
- "What if you use it with someone else — two people compare their vectors on the same problem? Not competitive. Complementary. 'You went left, they went up. Together you covered territory neither could alone.'"
- "What if the first time is SHOCKING? You think you're divergent — the tool shows you you're dead center. The 'oh shit' moment isn't 'wow I'm unique' — it's 'oh no, I'm average.' And THAT drives the change."
- "What if the interaction is just: make something, offer it to the tool, and watch it bloom or flatten? No numbers, no text. Pure visual/spatial response. Bloom = divergent. Flatten = vanilla."
