# 3-Minute Narration Script

## Budget allocation (Johnny verbatim 2026-04-25)

> *"The overall story of it should probably take 30 or 40 seconds because we are going to make it more high quality with a really strong good voiceover and the rest of it should be like an actual demo part of it."*

| Section | Time | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Black Mirror narrative setup | **0:00 – 0:35** (~35s) | Tone-set the future vision + contrast against current reality. Cinematic. |
| Problem statement + Third Option introduction | **0:35 – 0:55** (~20s) | Name the trilemma + the empowerment angle. |
| Demo — how it works + why it works | **0:55 – 2:35** (~100s) | Live demo with voiceover. Includes the 90s embedded `demo-script.md` arc. |
| Output / payoff / Best-Use-of-AI close | **2:35 – 3:00** (~25s) | What the user gets. The YEA/NAY argument. Tagline. |

**Total: ~180s. The first 35s is the cinematic spine. The middle 100s is the working demo. The closing 25s is the payoff.**

## ACT 1 — BLACK MIRROR SETUP (0:00 – 0:35)

### 0:00 – 0:08 — The future vision opening (REWRITE 2026-04-25 per Sophia validation pass 2)
**Visual:** Slow-motion shot of a teenager scrolling, illuminated only by the phone screen. Their face is unreadable. Behind them, fragments of feed content blur and bleed into each other. Sound: ambient hum, low strings, almost subaural.

**Voiceover (17-year-old voice, intimate, defeated — sets the inciting incident at desperation, not curiosity):**
> *"I've tried to think for myself. But every time I do, the algorithm pulls me back. I've given up trying."*

### 0:08 – 0:17 — The current reality reveal
**Visual:** Hard cut. Same teenager. Then a second teenager. Then a third. Then a wide shot — twenty teenagers, different faces, all scrolling, all the same posture, all reacting to the same content beat at the same micro-second. The Reels frames sync up across all twenty screens. The synchronization is uncanny.

**Voiceover:**
> *"Instead, everyone is converging to the same point. Your Spotify is no longer your music taste — it's a recommendation algorithm. Your YouTube isn't yours. Your Reels — you can't select any of that."*

### 0:17 – 0:27 — The structural failure named
**Visual:** Quick cuts of corporate logos morphing into data-extraction icons. A graph of "personalization adoption" climbing. Below it, a graph of "perceived control" flatlining. The two graphs cross.

**Voiceover:**
> *"The platforms that called themselves expressions of your taste are recommendation algorithms wearing the costume of personalization. The genitive is a lie. And the more we let them know us, the less we know ourselves."*

### 0:27 – 0:35 — The trilemma + BCI extension + third-option tease (UPDATED 2026-04-25 future-angle yap)
**Visual:** Two doors appear. Door 1: a person alone in a dim room, looking out at the world, falling behind. Door 2: a person whose silhouette is filled with corporate logos, hollowed-out behind the eyes. Quick flash-forward — same two figures, but now one has a visible BCI / brain-chip and the same hollowed-out eyes; the other is unmodified and being passed by. The pattern repeats, escalated. Then a third door materializes between them — bright, distinct.

**Voiceover (shift in tone — slight uplift):**
> *"You have two options. Don't use it — and fall behind. Or use it — and give up everything that makes you you. Today it's the algorithm. In five years it's brain chips. Same trade. Same trap. We came to build the third option."*

## ACT 3 — THE DEMO (0:55 – 2:35) — 100 seconds

> This section adapts the locked 90-second [`demo-script.md`](./demo-script.md) into the narration register. The visual cues stay; the voiceover is *narration-quality* (third-person, cinematic, explanatory) rather than *demo-on-stage* (second-person, moment-to-moment).

### Scene 1 — The Hook (0:55 – 1:10) — Recognition + Awe (REWRITE per Quinn validation pass 2 — strip reverse-inference)

**Visual:** Split-screen. Left: smartphone screen-recording of Reels scroll (trending dance, AI fashion advice, true-crime, relatable-sadness). Right: a 3D cortical mesh appears, initially dark gray, then begins glowing as the feed plays. Hot-pink-to-yellow gradients on emotion centers, default-mode network, visual cortex. Each scroll triggers a different region pulse.

**Voiceover (observational, not diagnostic — no causal-felt-emotion claims):**
> *"This is your feed. And this is the predicted neural response — across twenty thousand cortical points, second by second. Every region that lights up is a measurable response to attention-grabbing cues. Not a feeling we infer. A pattern we observe."*

### Scene 2 — The Body, Swarm Specialists (1:10 – 1:35) — Cross-talk made visible (REWRITE per Quinn — observational language)

**Visual:** Knowledge-graph nodes expand around the brain. Per-region specialist labels appear: *"Threat-detection node," "Memory-recall node," "Visual-attention node"* — function-named, not anatomy-named. A user hand hovers over the threat-detection region. Bridges light up between regions — yellow-orange edges with observation fragments flowing along them: *"recognized salience cue," "rapid-cut response," "social-signal pattern."*

**Voiceover:**
> *"Each region becomes a specialist agent — running in parallel, only fast enough to be live because of K2's thirteen-hundred-tokens-per-second speed. They observe what your region responded to, and they exchange notes. Hover any region — watch the cross-talk surface, on demand. The system's reasoning is auditable. Two layers un-black-boxed at once: what the algorithm chose for you, and what the AI is concluding about your response."*

### Scene 3 — The Pattern Repetition (1:35 – 1:55) — Listen Labs phenomenon visible (REWRITE per Maya + Quinn — pattern-repetition language; divergence-possibility lands ALONGSIDE, not after)

**Visual:** Three feed scrolls play in sequence. Each one activates the same brain regions in the same order. The pattern repeats, hauntingly identical — but on the side of the screen, a small ghost-trace shows alternative patterns the brain *could* fire if given different inputs. The viewer sees the lock AND the door at the same time.

**Voiceover (no shame-frame; pattern-recognition + immediate possibility):**
> *"Watch the same regions fire across every scroll, in the same sequence. Your brain has learned a rhythm — the algorithm taught it. This isn't broken. It's *adapted.* And what adapts can re-adapt. Because the same regions can fire to completely different content. We're about to show you what."*

### Scene 4 — The Toggle, The Surprise (1:55 – 2:15) — Same-subject divergence (REWRITE per Quinn — within-subject language, not population-norm)

**Visual:** Same brain. Different input. The user views a hand-curated short film — content not designed to grip them. The brain re-runs. Visibly different pattern: prefrontal up (38% → 92%), default-mode down (92% → 61%), insula bright (12% → 78%). Side-by-side cortical meshes appear, both labeled *"your brain."*

**Voiceover:**
> *"Same brain. Different input. The same regions, the same person — firing in a completely different pattern. The lock the algorithm set wasn't structural. It was learned. Your brain *can* fire any way you let it."*

### Scene 5 — The Land / Hero (2:15 – 2:35) — The empowerment moment

**Visual:** Spotify-Wrapped-style cards swipe through (Top Brain Regions, Top Content Categories, Cohort Brain-Print). Then the hero card: the 3D brain returns with most regions grayed out, three regions glowing dim blue. The card prompt: *"Pick a Region You Didn't Use."* User clicks Broca's Area. Suggestion card appears: content **formats** that would activate it (long-form essays, poetry, language-learning, wordplay).

**Voiceover (the positioning moment):**
> *"We don't recommend anything to you — that's how the algorithm works. We give you accessible information so you can make your own judgment. Click on a region your feed never touched. See the kinds of content that would. The system informs. You choose."*

## ACT 4 — OUTPUT / PAYOFF (2:35 – 3:00) — 25 seconds

### 2:35 – 2:48 — The credibility chip + the YEA/NAY rubric
**Visual:** A frame showing the Clair de Lune precedent — *"60 seconds of music in. 90.4% emotion-center match out. Eight rounds of iteration. This works."* Then a clean two-column slide: YEA (make invisible visible / surface options / menial scale-work / synthesize signals) | NAY (recommend / extract / replace judgment / present-as-human).

**Voiceover:**
> *"The proof this works is shipped. Last month, this same engine generated text that produced ninety-point-four percent of Clair de Lune's brain pattern. We know how to use it. And we have an opinion about when AI should — and shouldn't — be used. The system you just watched is built entirely on the YEA side."*

### 2:48 – 3:00 — The close (REWRITE 2026-04-25 per Sophia — protagonist's voice closes the story, not narrator's)
**Visual:** Return to the teenager from the opening shot. They look up from the phone. The third door is open behind them. They take a step toward it. Brief flash: the BCI version of the same shot — same teenager, augmented but choosing. Tagline appears: *"You can't see how the algorithm shapes your thinking. We made it visible."* Then the deeper line: *"Manipulation only works in the dark."*

**Voiceover (the 17-year-old voice returns, transformed — desperation → ownership):**
> *"Today it's Reels. In five years it's brain chips. Same trade. Same trap. We're building the design pattern for human-AI partnership before the cognitive interface becomes invisible to me. This isn't another AI tool. It's the first product I get to use *on* the algorithm instead of being used *by* it. Manipulation only works in the dark. I just turned the lights on."*

**Final frame:** product name + team names + sponsor logos.

## Production notes

### Voiceover register
- **Acts 1 + 4:** *Black Mirror* narrator register — calm, solemn, slightly cinematic. Consider hiring a voice actor or using ElevenLabs with careful prompt-engineering. Johnny's stated quality bar: *"really strong good voiceover."*
- **Acts 2 + 3:** Slightly warmer, more present — this is the "guide" voice (per StoryBrand: customer-as-hero, system-as-guide). Still calm. Still in control. Not breathless.

### Visual production
- **Act 1:** Cinematic. High production value. Slow-motion. The teenagers-converging shot is the hero shot of Act 1 — invest in it.
- **Acts 2–3:** Real demo footage. Live or pre-cached — the live-with-pre-cache fallback strategy applies. **Use the 90s embedded demo from `demo-script.md` as the source footage.**
- **Act 4:** Return to cinematic register. The teenager-walking-toward-the-door shot is the close. Mirrors the opening.

### Music / sound
- **Act 1:** Low strings, ambient hum, almost subaural. *Black Mirror* / *Severance* / *Mr. Robot* sound-design reference.
- **Act 2:** Music drops out for the third-option tease. Silence for one beat before the architecture preview.
- **Act 3:** Soft descending synthesizer tones per region activation (per `demo-script.md`). Otherwise quiet.
- **Act 4:** Music returns, slightly uplifting — relief, not triumph.

### Pacing
- Acts 1 + 4 are time-locked — no slippage. ~35s setup, ~25s close. Total cinematic budget: ~60s.
- Act 3 is time-locked at ~100s. Map directly to demo-script.md beats 1-4 (BEAT-5 sponsor close is off-timer).
- Act 2 is the only flexible section — can compress to 15s if Act 1 runs over.
