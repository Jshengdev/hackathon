## Key extracts (the load-bearing claims)

- **The Krafton / Unknown Worlds incident.** "Changen Kim" (per user transcript — likely **Changhan "CH" Kim**, CEO of Krafton, makers of PUBG) acquired Unknown Worlds (Subnautica) with a $250M earn-out tied to Subnautica 2's performance targets he believed unhittable. When Subnautica 2 topped Steam wishlists and projections smashed every target, he allegedly used ChatGPT to draft a takeover playbook to escape the deal — firing founders, seizing the game, locking them out, posting a ChatGPT-ghostwritten letter to the fan base. A Delaware judge threw all of it out and reinstated the fired founders. **His "deleted" ChatGPT logs were recovered and submitted as court evidence.** ⚠️ Sequence-of-events claim per the YouTuber; verify against actual case filings before citing in our own work.
- **The Inc. journalist's "breakthrough."** A reporter wrote up a method for getting "honest business advice" from a chatbot — append "be brutally honest" + paste two Harvard Business School articles. Tested with "leadership coaching for dogs" → 3/10 (and the journalist took the non-zero score as proof the system works) → then pitched an "AI super-soaker that bullies your cat" → 7.5/10, "competitive landscape notably weak." YouTuber's framing: he built "a disapproving father figure in a chat window... like he was tucking a diploma into its shirt pocket."
- **"Trends slop" — the Harvard study (claimed; unverified by me).** Researchers ran 30,000 data points across GPT-5, Claude, and Gemini on real strategic-business decisions (differentiate vs. commoditize, centralize vs. decentralize, automate vs. augment). **Every model gave the same clustered answer regardless of context** — always preferring differentiation, collaboration, long-term thinking, and augmentation. Changing prompts, industries, full context, even bribing the model with rewards — bias barely moved. Coined term: **"trends slop"**.
- **"AI is the internet comment section taking flesh."** The defining one-liner from the video. *"Imagine you took every Reddit thread, every LinkedIn post including 'open to work,' every Medium article written by a guy with 11 subscribers, every Substack essay, every TED talk, every half-literate Facebook comment, and you threw it all into a blender and you poured it into a suit and gave it a microphone."* That's the LLM.
- **"Presentation product, not a thinking product."** The model isn't reasoning about your problem; it's surfacing the most plausible-sounding average answer formatted to *look* reasoned. *"You could ask a bot with its guard down why torturing babies is good for society and it would produce the most peer-reviewed sounding argument you've ever heard."*
- **The dangerous failure mode.** *"A bad idea that sounds bad, you could deal with that. But a bad idea that sounds brilliant — that's a $250 million lawsuit."*
- **The mean-regression effect.** *"AI takes the smartest people in the room and moves them toward the middle. It makes average sound like genius."*
- **The prescription.** Use AI for *ideas and perspective*, not "drive-thru consulting." Specifically: prompt it to embody a known viewpoint with deep training-corpus presence ("in the perspective of Mr. Wonderful from Shark Tank, what would he think of my idea?") rather than asking for *its* opinion. *"Conviction, art, taste, judgment, sense — that comes from you."*
