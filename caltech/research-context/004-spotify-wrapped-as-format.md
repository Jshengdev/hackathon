# Spotify Wrapped as a personalized-data-narrative format

**Source:** Spotify annual "Wrapped" campaign (2016–present). Public artifact, no canonical academic citation.

**One-line claim:** Spotify Wrapped is the dominant Gen-Z native format for "system shows you your own data back as a shareable, scroll-through, lightly-gamified narrative."

**Why we cited it:** Johnny chose this as the locked output shape (B in the output-shape comparison). Reasons named in the yap:
- Format is *personalized data narrative*, not a report ("not a report-report, but it's like the [data] that's... your age group, your top genre, what it says about your brain")
- Shareable like a social-media trend (Sideshift B2C close)
- Tells the user about themselves with cute graphics
- Played card-by-card, not consumed all-at-once
- Educational without feeling educational

**Format-pattern features Johnny pulled out:**

1. **Card-by-card scroll** — each card a single insight, full-screen, swipe to next
2. **Top-N rankings** — your top genre, top artist, top minutes-listened (in our analog: top brain region activated, top content category, top convergence cluster)
3. **Comparative percentile** — *"my age group was 81 or something like that"* — gives the user a frame of reference (in our analog: how your brain-fire pattern compares to your age cohort)
4. **Cute graphics** — illustrative, not infographic-dense
5. **Shareable card export** — designed to land on Stories / TikTok
6. **Educational without lecturing** — *"trying to educate people with their neuroscience because the problem was that everyone's just a copy of each other"*

**Adjacents in the same format space (Gen-Z native):**
- **BeReal** "your year in moments" — annual recap + dual-camera authenticity frame
- **TikTok analytics** ("your 2024") — session/year-level engagement traces
- **YouTube Recap** — annual playback summary
- **Apple Screen Time / Wellness** — usage-replay (Gen-Z reads it but skips actioning)
- **Instagram Year-in-Review** stories

**Anti-pattern adjacency:** an "infographic" or "report" with the same data fails because it's not card-by-card and not shareable. Same data, wrong wrapper, wrong audience.

**Open question this format raises** (held; for stage 4 internal FAQ): *Can a 90-second hackathon demo render even 4-5 Wrapped-style cards convincingly, or does the format require longer playback?* Spotify's actual Wrapped is ~90 seconds of cards — so the format budget IS our budget. Map 1:1.
