# BeReal as the anti-curation pattern

**Source:** BeReal app (Alexis Barreyat, launched 2020). Public artifact.

**One-line claim:** BeReal's product thesis is "you can't lie about what you actually look like / do" — designed as the inverse of Instagram's curated-aspirational shape.

**Why we cited it:** Johnny floated BeReal as the candidate for the Sideshift B2C consumer close — *"the new BeReal — BeReal was fighting against fake social media; this could be fighting against the algorithm."* On reflection in the canvas yap, Johnny named the BeReal duality framing as harder to visualize and folded the *thesis* of BeReal (you can't lie about what you consume) into the Spotify Wrapped frame instead.

**The thesis seed that survived (folded into Wrapped frame):**

> *"You can't lie about what you consume anymore. You can't say 'oh I get all the cat reels' or whatever — it's just, you get this type of reel. It's like your thinking, we can summarize it. Your friends can see what you actually think."*

This is the **anti-aspirational** frame. The output shows you what the algorithm actually shaped you into, not what you'd post. Translates to the Spotify Wrapped output as: *the brain-fire pattern is the truth, irrespective of what the user thinks they consume.*

**What we DIDN'T pull from BeReal (rejected for hackathon scope):**
- Dual-camera "what you see vs what others see you doing" mechanic — too hard to visualize for our brain-data input
- Daily-prompt forced-share mechanic — out of scope for a single demo

**Status:** held as inspirational reference, not active design pattern.
