# Step 3: The Inputs

## STEP GOAL:

Resolve: what does the tool actually OBSERVE to see your thinking? It needs to SEE divergence somehow — what is it watching?

## MANDATORY EXECUTION RULES (READ FIRST):

### Universal Rules:

- NEVER auto-answer — facilitate the yap
- ENFORCE anti-patterns: if "tracking" language creeps in, kill it
- Teri's rule applies: "Remove the word tracker." Whatever the input is, it can't feel like surveillance.

### Step-Specific Rules:

- Reference the locked sentence from Round 1 to ground this discussion
- The input method must MATCH the reference point

## CONTEXT BOUNDARIES:

- Input: Locked reference point from Step 2
- Output: One locked sentence describing what the tool observes
- Dependencies: Step 2 complete

## MANDATORY SEQUENCE

### 1. Frame the Round

"**Round 2: The Inputs**

We know what divergence is measured against: [read back locked sentence from Round 1].

Now — the tool needs to SEE your thinking. What is it watching?"

### 2. Facilitate Yap (Use Prompts as Needed)

Present ONE AT A TIME. Wait for team yap.

**Prompt A:** "Your creative OUTPUT? — the designs, writing, code, decisions you produce. The tool analyzes the artifact."

**Prompt B:** "Your PROCESS? — the path you took, the references you pulled, the detours you made. Not the destination, the journey."

**Prompt C:** "Your CONVERSATIONS? — ideas you throw out, connections you draw, the lateral leaps in how you talk about problems."

**Prompt D:** "Your CONSUMPTION? — what you look at, read, reference, pull from. Where your inputs come from reveals where your thinking goes."

**Prompt E:** "Is it observing in THE MOMENT, or analyzing ARTIFACTS after the fact? Real-time sensing vs. retrospective reflection?"

**Prompt F:** "Remember Teri's rule: 'Remove the word tracker.' Whatever this is, it can't feel like surveillance or logging. How does it observe without watching?"

### Divergent Seeds (drop these if conversation stalls)

- "What if the tool doesn't observe YOU at all — what if it observes the SPACE between you and AI? The gap itself is the input."
- "What if it reads the hesitation — the moment you almost said something then didn't? The deleted text, the abandoned sketch, the idea you killed before it lived."
- "What if the input is your TASTE — not what you make, but what you choose. What you click, what you linger on, what makes you stop scrolling."
- "What if it observes the RHYTHM of your thinking — not the content but the pattern? Fast-fast-fast-slow... that slow moment is where divergence lives."
- "What if you feed it two things: what you made, and what AI would have made from the same prompt. The tool only sees the delta."
- "What if the tool observes NOTHING until you give it something? Not passive surveillance — active offering. You show it your move, and it shows you where that move lives."

### 3. Extract Signal

Listen for:
- What input method creates the richest vector visualization
- What feels natural vs. invasive
- What Kaitlyn connects to ("I just want the AI to tell me about my life")
- What preserves the "mirror not guide" principle

Reflect back what you're hearing.

### 4. Anti-Pattern Check

- Does this feel like data collection or reflection?
- Would the user feel watched or seen?
- Is the input method invisible/ambient or does it require active logging?
- Does it align with the reference point from Round 1?

### 5. Lock the Sentence

"**Time to lock Round 2.**

The tool observes:

[Propose a single sentence synthesized from the yap]

Does this capture it?"

Iterate until agreed. Write the locked sentence.

Append to `{outputLocation}/yaps/yap-03-inputs.md`

"**Round 2 locked.** We know what divergence is measured against AND what the tool observes.

Next: the big design question — what FORM does this thing take?"

## SYSTEM SUCCESS/FAILURE METRICS

### SUCCESS:
- Input method explored from multiple angles
- Aligns with locked reference point
- Passes anti-pattern check (not surveillance, not tracking)
- ONE sentence locked
- Feels like "being seen" not "being watched"

### FAILURE:
- Input method feels like data logging
- Doesn't connect to the reference point
- "Tracker" energy despite the concept rejecting trackers
- No consensus

**Master Rule:** The input must feel like looking in a mirror, not being recorded by a camera.
