# Step 6b: Competition Frame

## STEP GOAL:

The vision is locked. The concept is pure. Now — pressure-test it against every competition requirement before building the story. This step TRANSLATES the vision into competition language without diluting it. Fill gaps. Ground the concept. Give judges everything they need to score high on Problem-Solution Fit while keeping Innovation & Creativity untouched.

## MANDATORY EXECUTION RULES (READ FIRST):

### Universal Rules:

- This is FRAMING, not changing. The vision doesn't move. The frame wraps around it.
- Every gap filled here becomes raw material for the story arc
- If framing a requirement starts to warp the concept, STOP. The concept wins. Frame harder, not differently.
- Anti-patterns still apply — if "audience definition" starts sounding like a persona deck, kill it

### Step-Specific Rules:

- Read the full vision.md before starting
- Go through each competition requirement sequentially
- Lock each answer before moving on
- The team yaps, you extract — same as vision rounds

## CONTEXT BOUNDARIES:

- Input: Locked vision.md, competition requirements
- Output: `{outputLocation}/competition-frame.md` — all required sections filled
- Dependencies: Step 6 complete

## COMPETITION REQUIREMENTS MAP

The judges will look for **six required sections** in your submission. Every one must be addressed or you leave points on the table.

## MANDATORY SEQUENCE

### 1. Load and Ground

Read `{visionFile}`. Read the vision statement aloud.

"**The vision is locked. It's not changing.**

Now we frame it for judges. FigBuild requires six specific things in every submission. Some we've already nailed. Some we haven't addressed yet. Let's fill the gaps so the story has everything it needs."

---

### SECTION 1: WHO IS THIS FOR?

### 2. Frame the Audience

"**Required: 'Define your target audience and user group (age, context, needs).'**

We have a concept about divergent thinking. Who NEEDS this most?

Not 'everyone.' Not 'creatives.' A specific group with a specific pain."

### 3. Facilitate Yap

**Prompt A:** "Who is LOSING the most right now because AI is making the average free? Whose value is being erased? Designers? Writers? Strategists? Students?"

**Prompt B:** "Who ALREADY has divergent thinking but can't see it or prove it? People who get told 'that's a weird idea' and can't articulate why it's valuable?"

**Prompt C:** "Is this for individuals or teams? Does divergence matter more in isolation or in groups?"

**Prompt D:** "Age and context — is this for someone early in their career who hasn't learned to trust themselves yet? Or a veteran who's been converging for decades and lost their edge?"

### Divergent Seeds — Audience:

- "What if the audience is people who THINK they're average? The tool's power isn't confirming divergence — it's revealing it in people who never knew they had it."
- "What if it's for the person who just got their work replaced by AI-generated output and is asking 'what am I for?' This tool answers that question."
- "What if the audience is a PAIR — a human and their AI copilot? The tool shows the gap between them and makes the human's divergence the most valuable signal in the collaboration."

### 4. Lock Audience

"**Lock the audience.**

This is for: [propose 1-2 sentences — who, age/context, what they need]

Does this feel specific enough that you could picture one person?"

Iterate. Lock.

---

### SECTION 2: WHAT NEW SENSE?

### 5. Frame the Sense

"**Required: 'Identify the sensory experience you're addressing. Explain the new or heightened sense and why it matters.'**

Our sense is divergence — the distance between your thinking and the average. This is already strong. But let's make it airtight.

The competition mentions 22-33 human senses beyond the traditional five. Our claim is that divergence IS a sense — one that's always existed but never had an interface."

### 6. Facilitate Yap

**Prompt A:** "Is divergence closer to proprioception (knowing where your body is in space) or interoception (sensing your internal state)? It's like... knowing where your THINKING is in idea-space."

**Prompt B:** "What's the biological analogy? Proprioception for thought. Taste for originality. Echolocation for the edges of the average."

**Prompt C:** "Why does this sense matter NOW specifically? What changed in 2025-2026 that makes this the moment?"

### 7. Lock the Sense

"**Lock the sense definition.**

The sense: [propose 1-2 sentences — name it, define it, say why it matters now]

This should make a judge nod and think 'I've never heard it put that way.'"

Iterate. Lock.

---

### SECTION 3: WELLNESS GOAL

### 8. Frame Wellness

"**Required: 'Clearly define the wellness goal or behavior change your project is addressing. Consider which dimension of wellness it relates to.'**

The dimensions are: emotional, mental, physical, social, financial, environmental.

Our concept lives in MENTAL and EMOTIONAL wellness. But let's be specific about the change."

### 9. Facilitate Yap

**Prompt A:** "Is the wellness goal about CONFIDENCE? Trusting your own divergent instincts instead of self-censoring?"

**Prompt B:** "Is it about AWARENESS? You can't grow what you can't see. The tool makes your thinking patterns visible."

**Prompt C:** "Is it about RESILIENCE? When everyone converges and you don't, that's lonely. The tool shows you: your path still arrives. You're not wrong — you're divergent."

**Prompt D:** "Is there a behavioral change component? Like — after using the tool, people stop killing their 'bad ideas' and start exploring them?"

### Divergent Seeds — Wellness:

- "What if the wellness goal is ANTI-burnout? Burnout isn't from working hard — it's from working AVERAGE. The tool reconnects you with the thinking that feels like yours."
- "What if it's creative GRIEF? People who used to think divergently and lost it to algorithms, approval loops, optimization. The tool helps them find it again."
- "What if the wellness dimension is SOCIAL? The tool doesn't just show you YOUR divergence — it shows teams where they complement each other. Collaboration becomes 'I go left, you go up, together we cover territory neither could alone.'"

### 10. Lock Wellness Goal

"**Lock the wellness goal.**

The wellness goal: [propose 1-2 sentences — dimension + specific change]

Does this resonate as a real human need, not just a feature benefit?"

Iterate. Lock.

---

### SECTION 4: THREE USE CASES

### 11. Frame Use Cases

"**Required: 'Show 3 concrete use cases or stories demonstrating how the tool can be used in everyday life.'**

These should be SCENES, not feature descriptions. Three different people, three different contexts, same tool. Each should make a judge think 'I want that.'"

### 12. Facilitate Yap

"**Let's build three scenes. For each one:**
- Who's using it?
- What are they doing?
- What does the tool show them?
- What changes because of it?

**Scene 1** — the most obvious use case. The one that explains the tool fastest."

Wait for team yap. Shape into 2-3 sentences.

"**Scene 2** — the surprising one. A context nobody would expect this tool in."

Wait for team yap. Shape into 2-3 sentences.

"**Scene 3** — the emotional one. The scene that makes judges FEEL something."

Wait for team yap. Shape into 2-3 sentences.

### Divergent Seeds — Use Cases:

- "Scene 1: A designer using Figma Make. AI generates a clean layout. They made something messier, weirder. The tool shows: their version's vector arcs through territory the AI never touched. It still arrives at 'good design' — but it went somewhere new."
- "Scene 2: A job interview. Candidate gives an answer that's 'wrong' by conventional standards. The tool shows: it's a 1-in-10,000 path. The interviewer sees the arc and hires them."
- "Scene 3: A teenager who's been told they're 'not creative' their whole life. First time using the tool, they see their thinking pattern — it's not average at all. It's been divergent all along. Nobody ever showed them."

### 13. Lock Use Cases

Lock all three scenes. Each should be 2-3 sentences.

---

### SECTION 5: INFORMATION MANAGEMENT

### 14. Frame Information Design

"**Required: 'Design the interface for data collection and visualization, as well as the sensory perception/enhancement interface. Consider information overload, relevance, and when/how the app surfaces insights.'**

This connects directly to our locked TIMING and FORM from the vision. Let's translate those into the competition's language."

### 15. Facilitate Yap

**Prompt A:** "Our timing answer was: [read locked timing sentence]. How does this prevent information overload? When does the tool stay QUIET?"

**Prompt B:** "How does the tool decide what's RELEVANT to surface? Not every thought is divergent. What makes it light up vs. stay dim?"

**Prompt C:** "Is there a difference between the PASSIVE experience (ambient sensing) and ACTIVE experience (deep dive on demand)?"

### 16. Lock Information Design

"**Lock information management approach.**

[propose 2-3 sentences on how information is collected, surfaced, and managed]"

Iterate. Lock.

---

### SECTION 6: SAFEGUARDS

### 17. Frame Safeguards

"**Required: 'Address potential negative consequences and edge cases. Consider: privacy and consent, protection from misuse, emergency protocols, safeguards protecting both user and others.'**

This is where most teams phone it in. If we do this well, it shows MATURITY. Judges notice."

### 18. Facilitate Yap

**Prompt A:** "Privacy — what does the tool actually KNOW about you? What data exists, where does it live, who can see it?"

**Prompt B:** "Misuse — could someone weaponize divergence data? 'This employee thinks too differently.' How do we prevent that?"

**Prompt C:** "Mental health — what if someone sees they're NOT divergent and it crushes them? Or they become obsessed with being divergent? What's the guard rail?"

**Prompt D:** "Consent — does the tool observe passively or only when invited? How does opt-in work?"

### Divergent Seeds — Safeguards:

- "What if the safeguard IS the design philosophy? The tool never labels divergence as good or bad. It only shows shape, path, distance. No scores. No rankings. You can't weaponize a shape."
- "What if the data is EPHEMERAL? Your vectors exist only in the moment. No history stored. No profile built. Each session is fresh. You can't be surveilled by your own thinking patterns."
- "What if the biggest safeguard is that the tool shows you convergence TOO? It's not 'divergence = good.' Sometimes converging is exactly right. The tool shows you the landscape — you choose where to go."

### 19. Lock Safeguards

"**Lock safeguards.**

[propose 2-3 sentences covering privacy, misuse prevention, and design philosophy as protection]

These should feel like design decisions, not legal disclaimers."

Iterate. Lock.

## JUDGE AWARENESS

These judges are senior designers at Meta, Google, YouTube, Microsoft, Spotify, Patreon. They will notice:
- **Problem-Solution Fit** — is the audience real? Is the need grounded?
- **Safeguards** — most teams skip this. Addressing it shows design maturity.
- **Use cases** — concrete > abstract. Scenes > descriptions.
- **Wellness framing** — required by the prompt. Missing it = leaving points on the table.

## SYSTEM SUCCESS/FAILURE METRICS

### SUCCESS:
- All 6 competition requirements addressed with locked answers
- Vision unchanged — frame wraps around it, doesn't reshape it
- Audience is specific enough to picture one person
- Wellness goal feels like a real human need
- 3 use cases are SCENES, not feature descriptions
- Safeguards show design maturity, not checkbox compliance
- Team feels MORE confident about the concept, not less

### FAILURE:
- Frame diluted the vision to fit the requirements
- Audience is "everyone" or "creatives"
- Wellness goal is vague ("improve wellbeing")
- Use cases read like bullet points from a PRD
- Safeguards are boilerplate privacy policy language
- Team feels like they're filling out a form instead of building a world

**Master Rule:** The competition frame should make the concept feel MORE inevitable, not less. If framing weakens the vision, the frame is wrong — not the vision.
