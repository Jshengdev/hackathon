## Official
- **Prize:** YC Interview (1st), Partner Office Hours (2nd), Partner Office Hours (3rd)
- **Brief:** "Pick a YC company from before 2022 (i.e. from before ChatGPT came out), and build an AI-native MVP that reimagines or disrupts it for the world we live in now. This isn't about copying the original—this is about asking: 'If that company were founded today, what would it look like with AI in the driver's seat?'"
- **Link:** events.ycombinator.com/yc-hacktech-2026

## What They ACTUALLY Want (read between lines)
- **Highest reputational prize in the entire hackathon.** A YC interview is genuinely life-altering — winning this beats any cash prize except possibly Palohouse.
- The constraint "**before 2022**" is doing real work — it forces you to engage with companies that built the rails of the internet WITHOUT AI as an assumption. Then you re-architect with AI as a first principle.
- "**AI in the driver's seat**" — not AI as a feature. AI as the product, the differentiator, the moat.
- Implied: pick a company whose original 2010s thesis WAS great but whose execution can be 10x'd by AI. Don't pick a company that already failed for non-tech reasons.
- Saturday 4–5:30 PM YC Talk — attend or watch the recap. They'll signal-load there.

## How to Win
- **Pick a YC co with a clear 2010s thesis that obviously becomes better with AI agents** (avoid trying to AI-ify Airbnb or Stripe — too operational/regulatory)
- Strong candidates pattern: search engines, recruiting tools, sales tools, customer support, design tools, marketplaces with high search-cost, anything with manual judgment loops
- Build a **working product**, not a demo video. YC partners can sniff fakes.
- Lean hard on the "**reimagining**" angle in the pitch — be explicit about what the original got right and what AI changes
- Have a 30-second answer to "why is this defensible vs. the original company just adding AI"
