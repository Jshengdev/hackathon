# Step 9: Package & Ship

## STEP GOAL:

Assemble all deliverables for FigBuild 2026 submission. The story is already written. The design is built. This step is packaging — formatting what exists for delivery.

## MANDATORY EXECUTION RULES (READ FIRST):

### Universal Rules:

- SUBMIT EARLY. Edit later. DevPost accepts edits until deadline.
- The presentation IS the story — just formatted as slides
- Demo video narrates the story — just recorded over the prototype
- Do NOT reinvent the narrative at this stage — use what's locked

### Step-Specific Rules:

- Load story.md, vision.md, design-brief.md
- The Figma Slides structure maps 1:1 to the 5 story beats
- Submit rough Monday morning. Polish until 11PM ET Monday.

## CONTEXT BOUNDARIES:

- Input: All locked docs + completed Figma prototype
- Output: Submission-ready deliverables
- Dependencies: Design work complete (or in progress — submit what you have)

## MANDATORY SEQUENCE

### 1. Load All Docs

Read `{storyFile}`, `{visionFile}`, `{designBriefFile}`.

### 2. Figma Slides Plan

"**Figma Slides — presentation structure:**

The story IS the presentation. Map directly:

**Slide Section 1: The World** (1-2 slides)
- The inflection point
- Why now
- Set the emotional tone
- [Pull from Beat 1 prose]

**Slide Section 2: The Person** (1-2 slides)
- Meet the character
- The moment they dismiss a divergent thought
- Emotional hook
- [Pull from Beat 2 prose]

**Slide Section 3: The Encounter** (2-3 slides)
- EMBED the Figma prototype here
- The hero visualization
- First contact with the tool
- The 'wait, what?' moment
- [Pull from Beat 3 prose]
- This is where judges lean forward.

**Slide Section 4: The Shift** (1-2 slides)
- The invisible becomes visible
- The emotional payoff
- Before/after transformation
- [Pull from Beat 4 prose]

**Slide Section 5: The Vision** (1 slide)
- Scale — what changes for everyone
- The future worth building
- End with the big idea
- [Pull from Beat 5 prose]

**Total: 7-10 slides. No more.**

Embed Make prototypes in Section 3 for interactive demo."

### 3. Demo Video Plan

"**Demo video (< 5 min):**

**Structure:**
1. [0:00-0:30] The World — voiceover or text over mood visuals
2. [0:30-1:30] The Person — the scene, the dismissed idea
3. [1:30-3:30] The Encounter — SHOW the prototype. Screen capture the interaction. This is the meat.
4. [3:30-4:15] The Shift — the emotional payoff. What they see. How they feel.
5. [4:15-4:45] The Vision — the scale. The closing line.

**Production notes:**
- Screen-record the Figma prototype for Sections 3-4
- Voiceover narrates the story (use story.md prose)
- Let the design speak — minimal text overlays
- End on feeling, not features
- Background music: subtle, builds with the arc"

### 4. DevPost Submission Plan

"**DevPost text:**

Compress the 5-beat story into ~500 words:

**Title:** [Project name]

**Tagline:** [One line from vision statement]

**Description:**
- Paragraph 1: The World (Beat 1) — why this matters now
- Paragraph 2: The Concept (what the tool does, from vision statement)
- Paragraph 3: The Experience (Beats 3-4 compressed — what happens when you use it)
- Paragraph 4: The Vision (Beat 5 — where this goes)

**What it's built with:** Figma Design, Figma Make, [other tools]

**Screenshots:** Key frames from Beats 2, 3, 4

**Links:**
- Figma Slides (public link)
- Demo video"

### 5. Submission Timeline

"**Submission timeline:**

- **Monday morning:** Submit rough draft to DevPost. Rough video. Figma Slides link.
  - SUBMIT EARLY. You can edit until deadline.
- **Monday afternoon:** Polish Figma Slides. Re-record video if needed. Update DevPost.
- **Monday evening:** Final polish pass. Update DevPost description. Verify all links work.
- **Monday 11PM ET:** Deadline. Stop editing. Submit.

**The rule:** Submit at 10AM Monday. Everything after that is polish, not creation."

### 6. Pre-Submission Checklist

"**Before submitting, verify:**

- [ ] EDU verification confirmed for all 3 team members
- [ ] Figma Slides link is PUBLIC
- [ ] Prototype embedded and functional in slides
- [ ] Demo video uploaded and accessible
- [ ] DevPost description tells the story (not lists features)
- [ ] Screenshots are from hero moments (Beats 3-4)
- [ ] Project name is memorable
- [ ] Tagline is one sentence from the vision

**Category stacking check:**
- [ ] Main prize eligible
- [ ] Storytelling category ($1.5K) — is the narrative compelling?
- [ ] Innovation category ($1.5K) — is the concept genuinely new?
- [ ] Impact category ($1.5K) — does it support self-understanding?
- [ ] Design category ($1.5K) — is the craft exceptional?
- [ ] People's Choice — is it shareable?"

### 7. Final Validation

"**Final anti-pattern check:**

Read through all deliverables one more time:
- Does anything feel like a tracker? Kill it.
- Does anything feel like a dashboard? Kill it.
- Does anything prescribe behavior? Kill it.
- Does anything need a paragraph to explain? Redesign it.
- Does anything look borrowed? Replace it.

**The test:** Would a Figma CPO look at this and say 'this is why design matters'?"

### 8. Ship It

"**You built the world. You wrote the story. You made the design. Now ship it.**

Submit to DevPost. Share the Figma Slides link. Upload the video.

Then go get some sleep. Judging is March 16."

## SYSTEM SUCCESS/FAILURE METRICS

### SUCCESS:
- All deliverables planned with clear structure
- Presentation maps 1:1 to story beats
- Video plan tells the story, not features
- DevPost compresses the narrative effectively
- Submitted EARLY with time to polish
- All anti-patterns cleared in final check

### FAILURE:
- Presentation doesn't follow the story arc
- Video is a feature walkthrough
- DevPost is a bullet-point feature list
- Submitted at 10:59 PM in a panic
- Anti-patterns slipped through

**Master Rule:** Submit early. Edit later. The story is already told. Just ship it.
