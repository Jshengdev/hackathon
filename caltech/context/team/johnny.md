## Themes He Returns To (Evidence-Based From figbuild)

These are themes Johnny has personally written about, championed, or built into prior products. They represent the *intellectual territory he naturally returns to* when he picks problems.

### 1. Human-First, AI-Second (Sequencing Matters)
- Core thesis from figbuild's *Diverge*: "LLMs are optimized to CONTINUE thought, not generate new thought."
- "Creativity lives in the seed — and the seed must come from the human."
- Research grounding he repeatedly cites: MIT 2025 (83% couldn't recall their AI-assisted essays); human-first then AI = HIGHER brain connectivity than human-only.
- He has built entire products around enforcing this sequence (Diverge: human throws random word → AI builds path).

### 2. Un-Blackboxing AI / Making Cognition Visible
- The figbuild garden visualization is fundamentally an "un-blackbox" tool — paths show how thought is forming.
- "Show, don't tell" — the visualization IS the feedback. No labels, no judgments, no scores.
- He's drawn to architectures that **expose layers**: paths, branches, nodes, traces.

### 3. Cognitive Atrophy / "AI Sustainability for the Next Generation"
- Repeatedly cites: London taxi drivers (GPS shrank gray matter), doctors using AI 4 months → weakened cancer detection, Vygotsky's "thinking is talking," Socrates fearing writing.
- Vocabulary he uses: **"the dimming"** (felt reduction in cognitive effort), **"copilot becomes autopilot"** (invisible intellectual de-skilling), **"cognitive offloading"** (relinquishing thinking to machines without awareness).
- Frames this as *the* defining problem of his generation's relationship with AI.

### 4. Productive Resistance (Sweet-Spot Friction)
- From the TEDx work he cites: there's an unknown sweet spot of friction an AI should provide before people abandon it for something easier. *"Nobody's found it yet."*
- He builds tools that intentionally **make you work** — not informing, not assisting, requiring generation.
- Anti-pattern he avoids: dark UX patterns of constant validation / sycophancy / personalized tone.

### 5. Echoes vs. Fingerprints
- Vocabulary he uses heavily:
  - **Echo** = AI-typical output, weighted by training data, parallel to defaults
  - **Fingerprint** = human-unique output, weighted by lived experience, wide arc
  - **Thought Tree** = all possible thoughts as infinite branching paths
  - **Weighted Dice** = AI biased by training, human biased by lived experience — both meaningful, only one explores new territory
- The narrative pattern: you can't see the echoes from one path; you need the full picture to see the clustering.

### 6. Emergence Theory + Wolfram Complexity
- Has authored docs mapping Wolfram's complexity categories to UX (Cat 1=flat, Cat 2=echo, Cat 3=noise, Cat 4=fingerprint).
- "Move users from Cat 2 → Cat 4 *without* falling into Cat 3."
- Critical insight he holds: **raw randomness is NOT divergent thinking** — it's a different failure. High divergence + zero utility = noise.
- "Combinatorial explosion" + "computational irreducibility" + "butterfly effect" + "phase transitions" all in his vocabulary.

### 7. Growing, Not Building (Garden Over Architecture)
- "Design the rules, not the outcomes."
- The garden metaphor in his work isn't decorative — it's structurally how he describes emergent systems.
- User as **gardener**, not architect — they plant seeds and see what grows.
- Failed paths stay; dead ends are part of the data.

### 8. Reflection, Not Measurement
- "If it looks like a fitness app, delete it."
- "Stats, not scores. The user decides which stat matters."
- Strong anti-dashboard, anti-tracker, anti-quantification stance for cognitive/creative tools.
- "You don't count the trees in your forest to know it's grown — you feel it."

## Anti-Patterns Johnny Has Documented

Drawn directly from figbuild's design brief — these are anti-patterns he has explicitly refused to ship:

1. **No trackers.** Not tracking. Reflecting.
2. **No dashboards.** If it looks like a fitness app, delete it.
3. **No scores.** Not good/bad. Not a number. A shape.
4. **No prescription.** Never tells you what to think.
5. **No borrowed aesthetics.** Our taste. Our sauce.
6. **No AI-generated starting words / seeds.** The divergent input must be human.
7. **No rewarding noise.** Cat 3 scatter ≠ divergence.
8. **No killing emergence.** Too-prescriptive filters destroy genuine surprises.
9. **No hiding failures.** Dead-end paths are part of the garden.
10. **No labeling during the process.** End-of-session reveal only — "Show, don't tell."

## Patterns He Has Deliberately Designed In Past Work (For Reference)

From the *Diverge* PRD he co-authored — mechanics he has shipped and would carry forward:

- **The North Star is set first** — anchor point, always visible.
- **AI generates the baseline echo first** — so the user sees what "normal" looks like before they're asked to diverge.
- **Human-generated seed mandatory** — AI can't pick the starting word.
- **AI continues, never starts.** Strict sequencing.
- **All paths visible simultaneously** — no pagination, current path highlighted.
- **Adjust / Add / Branch on any node** — first-class interactions.
- **No win state, no labels, no scores during the process.**
- **End-of-session reveal** — visualization of how the path diverged from echoes; not a judgment, a record.
- **Export your garden** — visual snapshot + path data; the brainstorming process is the record.
- **All path data on-device** — no cloud, no comparison, no leaderboard.
