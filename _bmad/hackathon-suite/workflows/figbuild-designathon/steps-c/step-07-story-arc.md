# Step 7: Story Arc

## STEP GOAL:

Build a 5-beat narrative that serves as EVERYTHING: the design brief, the Figma Slides structure, the demo video script, and the DevPost description. One artifact, four uses. Each beat becomes a design moment AND a presentation section.

## MANDATORY EXECUTION RULES (READ FIRST):

### Universal Rules:

- Load vision.md first — the story emerges FROM the vision
- Each beat is written collaboratively, not auto-generated
- The team READS ALOUD after each beat — if it doesn't hit, rewrite
- The story is about a PERSON, not a product

### Role Reinforcement:

- You facilitate the narrative construction
- Push for specificity: names, scenes, feelings — not abstractions
- The story should make someone who has never heard the concept FEEL it

### Step-Specific Rules:

- Go through beats sequentially — each builds on the last
- Write each beat as 2-4 sentences of narrative prose
- After each beat, note the design implication (what this becomes in Figma)
- Anti-pattern: if a beat starts explaining features, kill it. Stories show, they don't explain.

## CONTEXT BOUNDARIES:

- Input: Locked vision.md
- Output: story.md with 5 beats + design implications
- Dependencies: Step 6 complete

## MANDATORY SEQUENCE

### 1. Load Vision

Read `{visionFile}` completely. Internalize the vision statement and all 5 pillars.

### 2. Frame the Story Phase

"**Phase 3: Build the Story**

The story has 5 beats. Each becomes a design moment AND presentation slides.

This is not a product description. This is a NARRATIVE. A person, a moment, a change. Judges remember stories, not feature lists.

2025 FigBuild winners built narrative worlds. That's what we're doing now.

We'll write each beat together, read it aloud, and move on when it hits."

---

### BEAT 1: THE WORLD

### 3. Facilitate Beat 1

"**Beat 1: The World**
*Why now. What's happening. The inflection point.*

Write 2-3 sentences that make someone FEEL the urgency. Not facts — feeling.

Think about:
- AI is making the average free
- Everything converging toward vanilla
- The most valuable human quality — thinking differently — is invisible
- The cost of finding answers is zero. The cost of original thought is everything.

What does this world FEEL like to live in?"

### Divergent Seeds — Beat 1:

- "What if the world isn't dystopian — it's COMFORTABLE? Everyone's fine. Everything works. And that's the horror — it all works because nobody's thinking differently anymore."
- "What if you frame it through a specific moment: the day an AI won a design award and the judges couldn't tell. Not scary. Just... quiet. The average became invisible."

Wait for team input. Shape into 2-3 sentences of prose.

**Design implication:** "This sets the tone, palette, and mood. It's the opening of your Figma Slides."

Read aloud. If it doesn't create urgency, rewrite.

---

### BEAT 2: THE PERSON

### 4. Facilitate Beat 2

"**Beat 2: The Person**
*Not a persona. A moment.*

Write a specific scene:
- Someone facing a problem or creative challenge
- They have a thought — a weird one, a 'bad idea,' a left-handed move
- They dismiss it because it doesn't match the 'right answer'
- That dismissed thought was their most valuable one

Give them a name. Put them in a place. Make it specific."

### Divergent Seeds — Beat 2:

- "What if the person isn't a designer or creative? What if they're a nurse, a teacher, a parent — someone whose divergent thinking is invisible because their field doesn't celebrate 'creativity'?"
- "What if the 'bad idea' they dismiss is actually something they've dismissed HUNDREDS of times? This isn't the first time. It's a pattern they can't see yet."
- "What if they don't dismiss it — someone ELSE does? A manager, a client, an algorithm that ranks their work as 'not engaging enough.'"

Wait for team input. Shape into a scene.

**Design implication:** "This creates the emotional entry point. The first frame in your prototype."

Read aloud. Do you care about this person? If not, rewrite.

---

### BEAT 3: THE ENCOUNTER

### 5. Facilitate Beat 3

"**Beat 3: The Encounter**
*First contact with the tool. The 'wait, what is this?' moment.*

Write the scene:
- The tool shows them something they've never seen before
- Their thinking path, visible for the first time
- The cluster of average responses vs. their own arc
- They see the dismissed 'bad idea' was actually the most divergent — and it still arrives at the goal

This is the HERO moment. The vector visualization. The thing that makes judges lean forward."

### Divergent Seeds — Beat 3 (push hard here, this is the hero beat):

- "What if the encounter is ACCIDENTAL? They weren't looking for this tool. It found them."
- "What if the first thing they see is NOT their own divergence — it's everyone else's CONVERGENCE? A sea of identical paths. And then they see their own, arcing away."
- "What if the dismissed 'bad idea' from Beat 2 is visualized as the most BEAUTIFUL vector in the entire field? The thing they killed was their masterpiece."
- "What if the encounter starts with the AI's version first — clean, perfect, vanilla — and then their version overlays on top and it's messy and wild and ALIVE?"

Wait for team input. Shape into the encounter scene.

**Design implication:** "This is the hero screen. The signature visualization. The moment the prototype exists for."

Read aloud. Is there a "wait, what?" moment? If not, rewrite.

---

### BEAT 4: THE SHIFT

### 6. Facilitate Beat 4

"**Beat 4: The Shift**
*What changes. The invisible becomes visible.*

Write the feeling:
- They see their own sauce for the first time
- Not a score. Not good/bad. A SHAPE. A path. A trajectory.
- They understand: the weird detour was the most valuable move
- They start to trust their divergence instead of suppressing it

This is the emotional climax."

### Divergent Seeds — Beat 4:

- "What if the shift isn't joy — it's grief? They see all the divergent paths they killed over the years. The shape of their lost originality. And THAT is what drives them to change."
- "What if the shift is physical? They feel it in their body — a loosening, an unclenching. They'd been holding their divergence tight without knowing it."
- "What if they don't just see their OWN shape — they see how their shape complements someone else's? Divergence isn't solo. It's symphonic."

Wait for team input. Shape into the shift.

**Design implication:** "This is the emotional payoff screen. The design should make you FEEL the shift — not explain it."

Read aloud. Does it move you? If not, rewrite.

---

### BEAT 5: THE VISION

### 7. Facilitate Beat 5

"**Beat 5: The Vision**
*Where this goes. What changes for everyone.*

Not 'imagine if millions used this' — that's generic.
What changes about HUMANITY if everyone could sense their own divergence?

Think about:
- Divergent thinking becomes visible and valued
- The 1-in-10,000 move isn't luck — it's trainable
- AI and human coexist: AI holds the average, humans explore the edges
- Everyone can see their own sauce and grow it intentionally

End with the big idea. Leave judges with something they'll think about after."

### Divergent Seeds — Beat 5:

- "What if the vision isn't about the tool at all? It's about a world where 'that's a weird idea' becomes a compliment. The tool is just the catalyst."
- "What if you end with the chess analogy full circle — the 1-in-10,000 move that nobody recognized until AI showed them. Now imagine every human can see their own 1-in-10,000 moments."
- "What if the closing line is a question, not a statement? Leave judges sitting with it."

Wait for team input. Shape into the vision close.

**Design implication:** "This is the final slide. The closing shot. Leave them wanting more."

Read aloud. Does it feel like a future worth building? If not, rewrite.

## SYSTEM SUCCESS/FAILURE METRICS

### SUCCESS:
- All 5 beats written as narrative prose (not bullet points)
- Each beat has a clear design implication
- Story reads as a cohesive arc when read top to bottom
- A person who has never heard the concept would FEEL it
- Team is moved by their own story
- story.md saved with all mapping notes

### FAILURE:
- Beats read as feature descriptions, not narrative
- No specific person (generic "users")
- No emotional arc (just information delivery)
- Team says "it's fine" instead of "it hits"
- Missing the "wait, what?" moment in Beat 3

**Master Rule:** If the story doesn't move YOU, it won't move judges. Rewrite until it does.
