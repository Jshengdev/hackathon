## ROUND 1 — 5-min deck (13 slides)

### 01 · Title
"Gen-Z has never had a reference frame outside the algorithm. Their thoughts converge with their feed. They cannot tell which thoughts are theirs. Today we show them how to see the difference. We made the algorithm visible."

### 02 · Trilemma
"You have two options. Don't use it — and fall behind. Or use it — and give up everything that makes you you. We came to build the third option."

### 03 · Convergence (today)
"Your Spotify is no longer your music taste — it's a recommendation algorithm. Your YouTube isn't yours. The genitive is a lie. The more the algorithm knows you, the less you know yourself."

### 04 · BCI scale (tomorrow)
"Today the algorithm hijacks your attention through your phone. Tomorrow it hijacks your cognition through your brain. Same architecture. Same problem. We came to build the design pattern for human–AI partnership before the cognitive interface becomes invisible to you."

### 05 · Empowerment (the third option)
"We built the first product designed for human–AI partnership where you keep the choice. Not a wellness app. Not a browser extension that recommends better content. A system where AI becomes your mirror, not your master."

### 06 · Architecture (4-layer)
"TRIBE V2 — Meta FAIR's brain-encoding model — predicts cortical activation across 20,000 vertices in real time. We split that into a swarm of specialist agents, one per brain region, talking at K2's ~1,300 tokens per second. Then Claude Opus does the synthesis. Then we visualize the whole thing so you can click into your own thinking."

### 07 · Hover-bridge
"Hover over any brain region and you see two things light up. The algorithm's choice — what it fed you to make that region fire. And the AI's reasoning about why. The cross-talk is visible on demand. Nothing hidden."

### 08 · Wrapped
"Five cards. The first four tell you what your thinking looks like — which regions are active, what you're consuming, where you match your peers. The fifth card is the hero. It's not 'here's what you should watch next.' It's 'click on a region you didn't use, and see what kinds of content would activate it.'"

### 09 · Land card
"Click a region your feed never touched. The system surfaces content **formats** that would activate it — long-form essays, poetry, language-learning, wordplay. Not recommendations. Ingredients. The inverted brain search."

### 10 · YEA / NAY
"This is our operating system. YEA: make the invisible visible. Surface options. Let the user judge. Synthesize signals. NAY: recommend. Replace judgment. Extract without consent. Fake being human. Every part of this system is built on the YEA side. That's the Best Use of AI argument. The system enacts the answer."

### 11 · Clair de Lune
"Last month we fed 60 seconds of Clair de Lune to TRIBE V2 and asked Claude to generate text that would produce the same brain pattern. Eight rounds. We hit 90.4% match in the emotion center. Region by region — emotion, memory, self-reflection — it matched. We tested against controls to make sure it wasn't just hitting emotion in general. It was Clair de Lune specifically. Shipped, dated, public."

### 12 · Category-creator close
"This is the design pattern for human–AI partnership before the cognitive interface becomes invisible. Today it's Reels. In five years it's brain chips. Same trade. Same trap. We're building the alternative. Manipulation only works in the dark. We just turned the lights on."

### 13 · Team + Ask
"Johnny on innovation and architecture. Junsoo on TRIBE V2 and brain encoding. Jacob on K2 swarm. Emilie on packaging and video. We built this in 48 hours. We ship Saturday night: a 90-second video, this deck, the code, the demo. What we need from you: [sponsor-specific ask]."
