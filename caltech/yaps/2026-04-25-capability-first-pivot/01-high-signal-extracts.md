# High-signal extracts — 2026-04-25 capability-first pivot

Johnny named these. Claude reflects sharper, dispatches research, doesn't propose.

## A. The pivot — capability-first, not outcome-first

> *"Maybe an easier process would be to start with what the tribe thing normally does and then also describe what it can do and then figure out what one of the can-dos can turn into something that we wanted to do."*

**The shift:** stop trying to invent execution layers in a vacuum. Start with the *primitives* TRIBE V2 + K2 swarm actually support, then choose which primitive composition produces the empowerment-shaped feature we want.

**Why this is sharper:** the previous TreeHacks pattern search returned 5 outcome-shaped adaptations. Johnny's instinct: *we don't yet know which ones the primitives support.* Better to map the primitive surface first, then pick.

## B. The "they already do this" insight — load-bearing

> *"Neuroscientists and other researchers already collect data and stats on what people watch recently, like trends in content."*

**Implication:** the *data-collection* layer is not novel. Stats-on-content-consumption are commodity. **Our novelty is not in *measuring* what was consumed; it's in what we DO with the measurement that's user-side instead of platform-side.**

This sharpens what to NOT pitch: don't pitch "we measure your content consumption" (commoditized). DO pitch "we apply the same brain model the platforms use against you, but for you."

## C. The reverse-direction inheritance — locked from prior turn but reframed here

Johnny restates the thesis as a *capability inheritance*:

> *"The original intention [of TRIBE V2] was the whole attention farming thing. Our thought thing is trying to find what else we can use that information for."*

**Operational frame:** TRIBE V2's industry use case is attention-engineering. Our product **uses the same model's outputs for the inverse function.** The capability is identical; the application reverses.

This is the cleanest possible articulation of *"we don't have novel tech — we have novel ethics with shared tech."* Defends against red-team RT-7 (K2-could-be-Claude legibility — but the brain model is the load-bearing piece, not the LLM).

## D. The hackathon-method gamble (Johnny self-aware)

> *"This is the worst way to come up with ideas... but name another scenario where you're able to fit every single sponsored track into one idea. Yes it's a huge gamble but it's the hackathon method because you have a higher chance of winning something than nothing."*

**The lock:** multi-track stacking is a deliberate strategy with known cost (idea may be diluted). Johnny knows this. **It's not a confused goal; it's a known-cost bet.**

This means: when validation agents flag "trying to satisfy too many sponsors muddies the value prop" (Mary's R1 attack), the response is *"we accept that critique; multi-track is the bet, not an accident."*

**Operational implication:** we should explicitly look for ideas where multi-track coverage is *natural* (each sponsor's brief maps to a different primitive composition of the same engine), not *forced* (sponsor-by-sponsor framing on top of an unrelated core).
