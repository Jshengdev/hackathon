3. **The best managers were always the ones with EQ + empathy.** Industries like construction deserve that too — without giving up AI productivity.
4. **The win-win triple** — workers, managers, AND the company benefit. Frame as "real way to solve problems is not brute force, it's understanding."

### Verbatim phrases — LOCKED

> "The Agent Swarm simulation is providing a new layer of data that allows AI to understand spatially the world around it so that we can make more human AI guided decision-making."

### The Ironsight example to use
- Construction footage (provided by Ironsight).
- The mechanism: "We discovered which parts of their brain fired a lot more often, and those could be attributed to higher cortisol levels and higher levels of stress." (Msg 10)
- The reframe: "The best way you can treat your workers isn't to try to optimize their time to get them to do more work, but to optimize their emotions so that they can be better humans and your managers can become better humans." (Msg 10)

### One-line close (Ironsight)

- *Recognition* that text-based persona simulation has been hitting a ceiling.
- *Awe* that we can simulate how neurons FIRE — actual thought paths, not text approximations.

- *Conviction* that this is the new substrate for what they've been trying to build.

1. **We don't simulate personas through text — we simulate how neurons fire.** Where thoughts go. What reactions look like to pieces of content.

3. **In order to have something understand you, you have to converge to a point where you no longer think outside of it.** That's the trap. Our system breaks it.
4. **When you make active decisions, places in your brain fire far better and cleaner than when you're just watching a reel.** Active thought has a measurable signature.
5. **The value beyond convergence** — what people would think if the algorithm didn't already think for them.
6. **Generational stake.** "Younger generations are losing their ability to think and curate their own ideas when everything is tailored an algorithm for what they want."

### Verbatim phrases — LOCKED
> "Instead of talking about how people normally simulate personas through text, we're trying to simulate how neurons fire — almost like where thoughts go and what reactions look like to pieces of content."

> "Showing them a feed of reels that we can ingest and understand how it's un-blackboxing the algorithm and allowing us to interact with content that we've never seen before."

> "In order to have something understand you, you have to converge to a point where you no longer think outside of it and you just consume and consume."

> "Be able to see and make decisions before they even happen — almost puts the YOU back in the driving seat."

> "What lies beyond the convergence of everybody else."

### The Listen Labs example to use

## ✦ PERSONA 3 — AI INTERACTION (best use of AI · creativity · design taste)

### What this audience should FEEL
- *Conviction* that AI-as-augmentation is meaningfully different from AI-as-replacement.
- *Recognition* that "generate, generate, generate" workflows are losing them their divergent thought.
- *Empowerment* that taste — the one skill that persists beyond AI — can stay accessible.
- *Aesthetic clarity* about creativity, not "sexy" / hype-y / buzzword energy.

### Hard-hitters to land
1. **Most people right now: generate, generate, generate.** That's the problem.
2. **Specific steps to LEVERAGE AI, not just utilize AI.** Sequence matters.
3. **Our solution NEVER makes the decision for you.** It simulates your reactions and presents them so YOU make the divergent thought.
4. **Taste is the one skill that persists beyond AI.** And only if it stays accessible.
5. **Feeling is the best way to understand whether you're converging outside of something** — or whether you're communicating an emotion you've experienced.
6. **The autumn-leaves cosine example.** Want a design to feel like autumn leaves? Feed both → cosine similarity tells you whether your work lands.
7. **Human-to-human connection WITH the use of AI** — not REPLACED by AI generating things to fill the gap.

### Verbatim phrases — LOCKED
> "Most people right now, they just generate, generate, generate."

> "You should be able to have specific steps to leverage AI instead of utilizing AI."

> "Never making decisions for you, simulating your data and your reactions and trying to present them in a way where you're the one who's making the ultimate divergent thought."

> "Taste is the one skill that persists beyond AI. And the only way for it to persist is by having it accessible to most people."

> "If you had wanted to put a video of autumn leaves or something smooth and something beautiful, and then compare that with whatever you want to design, the taste — see if they match the same cosine similarity between what was tested and what was used."

> "Use it as a tool to have a specific vision of an environment and a feeling and translate that into whatever you want to design — and release that into a world where people can interact with it the same way that you felt."

> "Human-to-human connection with the use of AI, and not replacing human-to-human connection by generating things in an add-out filling the gap."

### The AI Interaction example to use
- Design / taste — the loud-professor lecture clip OR the autumn-leaves cosine match.
- Mixed-toward-Reels but framed as design-first.
- The cosine-matching workflow: reference video (autumn leaves / smooth / beautiful) → your draft → cosine match → ship if it lands.

### Voice / register — IMPORTANT
- **NOT "sexy."** Lean into thoughtful, considered, human-creativity-first.
- Avoid hype words ("revolutionary", "game-changing", "next-gen").
- Plain confident voice. The pitch IS the artistic choice.

### What NOT to say to this audience
- ❌ Anything that sounds like another GenAI product pitch.
- ❌ Productivity / efficiency framing — that's the wrong axis.
- ❌ Sponsor-specific framing — this is the universal / general use of the engine.

### One-line close (AI Interaction)
> "AI is meant to augment human creativity, not replace it. Taste persists beyond AI — only if it stays accessible. Now it does."
