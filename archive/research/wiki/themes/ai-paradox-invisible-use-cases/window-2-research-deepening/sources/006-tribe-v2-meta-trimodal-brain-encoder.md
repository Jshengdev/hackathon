## Key extracts (the load-bearing claims)

### What it is
- **Tribe V2** = Meta's **trimodal brain encoder**. Feed it video, audio, and text; it predicts how a human brain would respond to that stimulus across **70,000 voxels** of fMRI activity.
- **Architecture (3 stages):**
  1. **Per-modality encoders.** **V-JEPA 2** (video) + **Wav2Vec2-BERT** (audio) + **Llama 3.2 3B** (text transcript). Each emits a dense moment-by-moment numerical description.
  2. **Central transformer** fuses the three streams (same basic architecture family as GPT) — learns how the three modalities interact.
  3. **Voxel mapper.** Projects the merged representation onto **70,000 individual voxels** across the whole brain.
- **Training scale.** V2: **700 subjects**, **500+ hours** of fMRI, **1,100+ hours** of stimulus material. (V1: 4 subjects, 1B params, ~1,000 brain areas.) **70× resolution** vs V1; **175× subjects**.
- **Performance.** **54% of explainable brain variance** explained at 70K-voxel resolution. **2–3× better than previous methods** for movies and audiobooks.
- **Zero-shot generalization.** Predicts brain activity for *new people* it's never trained on, *new languages*, *new content types*. Median prediction is closer to the *group average* than most individual subjects' real scans (because individual scans contain artifact noise — heartbeat, head motion, attention drift).
- **Data-efficient fine-tune.** **~1 hour** of new-subject fMRI → 2–4× boost over baseline linear models trained from scratch on the same person.
- **Scaling law confirmed.** More fMRI data → log-linear improvement, **no plateau in sight**. Same shape as GPT/Claude/Gemini scaling curves, but for brain prediction.
- **Won Algonauts 2025** competition (out of 263 teams). MIT, Universität Berlin, Goethe University–Frankfurt organizers.
- **Published openly.** Model weights, full GitHub codebase, **ICLR 2026 paper**, interactive demo. **Non-commercial license** on the model itself.
- **Lead researcher:** Jean-Rémi King, CNRS neuroscientist on detachment at Meta Paris.

### What it surfaces about the brain
- **Functional networks emerge in the model's last-layer ICA components** — primary auditory, language, motion, default-mode, visual systems. The model **independently reverse-engineered the brain's large-scale functional architecture**.
- **In-silico replication of canonical findings.** Hemodynamic-response timing peaks at ~5s; FFA lights up for faces, PPA for places, EBA for bodies; left-hemisphere lateralization + Broca's-area surge for syntactically-complex sentences. All recovered without targeted training.
- **Multi-modal integration is biggest gain at the brain's known multi-sensory hubs** — temporal-parietal-occipital junction. Visualizing modality contributions as RGB on cortex shows pure colors at single-modality regions and **mixed colors (e.g. yellow at superior temporal lobe) at known bimodal-processing zones**.

### What it explicitly is NOT
- **Does NOT read your private thoughts.** Predicts brain *reactions to external stimuli*, not internal monologue, intention, emotion-from-the-inside.
- **Does NOT explain everything.** **46% of explainable variance remains unpredicted.** Lots of unknown territory.
- **Trained on movies in scanners** — controlled lab; "real world is messier" (Thomas Zoega Ramsoy, neuromarketing researcher, cautioning against reverse inference).
- **Slow modality.** Bound by fMRI temporal resolution.
- **Static adult brain.** No development, no disease, no plasticity.
- **Missing senses.** No smell, no touch.
- **Passive observer.** Brain modeled as receiver, not active agent.

### Privacy / regulatory context
- Multiple US states passing **neural-data privacy laws** in flight.
- UN Special Rapporteur **Ana Brian Nougrères** has flagged brain-prediction tech for manipulation potential.
- Released non-commercial license, but model + code are public — diffusion is irreversible.

## How this maps to the theme

This is the **technical anchor** for the "augmented intelligence" half. Three concrete adds:

1. **A tool that can pre-test what content does to a human brain *before* it's published.** That is — by literal architectural fact — *the inverse of trends slop*. Trends slop happens because models output the corpus mean and content gets shipped without anybody knowing what it'll do. Tribe V2 is the first publicly-available system that lets you check the predicted neural response to a piece of content *in software*, before deployment.
2. **A scaling-law pattern that says brain-prediction is going to keep getting sharper** for years. Anything we build on top of it inherits that improvement curve.
3. **Multi-modal integration as a brain-native primitive.** If the bet is "AI augmentation should mirror the structure of the human mind it's augmenting" (per source 005), the brain-native shape per Tribe V2 is *integrated audio + visual + linguistic*, with biggest gains at the multi-sensory junction. That's a *prescriptive* signal for what an augmented-intelligence product's modality stack should look like.

Bridges to existing theme vocabulary:
- *"Filter World"* (source 002) — Tribe V2 is the first credible candidate for a *neural-response filter* on the things the algorithm wants to feed you. Pre-screen for slop before scroll.
- *"Trends slop"* (source 003) — currently we have no measurement; Tribe V2 *is* a measurement instrument.
- *"Emotivist layer"* (source 004) — was hand-waved in source 004 as "a vibe checker that classifies claim type." Tribe V2 is a much sharper instrument: classify what neural-response signature the content will trigger.
- *"Mind as gap"* (source 005) — the gap is where consideration happens; Tribe V2 lets you put a *measured* prediction inside that gap before the user reacts.

## Cautions before leaning all the way in

- **Non-commercial license** on Meta's release. For a hackathon-stage demo this is fine; a *real* product would need a license check.
- **Reverse-inference risk** is the largest scientific failure mode (correctly invoked by Ramsoy). If a region lights up, you cannot reliably infer the felt experience. Don't build a UX claim that requires reverse inference.
- **"Reading your brain" is the hype framing the marketing wants** — the model only predicts response to *external* stimuli. Be precise in product copy.
- **fMRI is slow** — Tribe V2's predictions are at fMRI's temporal resolution (seconds, not milliseconds). Whatever we build has to be OK with second-scale latency on the brain-prediction layer.
- **Privacy.** Building consumer-facing brain-prediction product crosses into the territory the UN special rapporteur is already raising flags on. There are good versions (creator pre-screening their own content) and bad versions (advertisers pre-screening targeting). Pick the good version explicitly.

## Sharpening questions (Socratic — not answers)

- *Where does Tribe V2 sit in our stack?* — at ingest (predict response to a candidate piece of content)? at composition (let a creator iterate until predicted response matches intent)? at consumption (warn the user before they scroll into a high-arousal content stream)? Each is a different product.
- *What's the user's concrete benefit in 30 seconds?* — Tribe V2 outputs a 70,000-voxel vector. What's the human-legible artifact downstream? A score? A heatmap? A "this content will make you feel X" sentence? The visualization is the demo.
- *Does using Tribe V2 require us to put anyone in a scanner?* — answer per the transcript: no, it's a *predictor*. So our UX never touches a real fMRI machine. That's the key affordance: brain-prediction without the $3M scanner. Build for that.
- *Who else is using Tribe V2 right now?* — search GitHub forks, X mentions, ICLR 2026 follow-up papers. Field is small; we want to know who else is in it.
