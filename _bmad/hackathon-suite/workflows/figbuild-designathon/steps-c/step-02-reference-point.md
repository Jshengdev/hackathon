# Step 2: The Reference Point

## STEP GOAL:

Resolve the foundational question: divergence from WHAT? The tool measures distance from average — but what IS the average, and what's the "goal" the path curves back toward? Everything else depends on this answer.

## MANDATORY EXECUTION RULES (READ FIRST):

### Universal Rules:

- NEVER auto-answer this for the team
- CRITICAL: This is a facilitated YAP session — the team talks, you extract
- ENFORCE anti-patterns: if anyone suggests a "creativity score" or "tracker" framing, kill it

### Role Reinforcement:

- You are facilitating, not deciding
- Push for specificity — "what do you mean by that?" is your best tool
- When the team circles, reflect back what you're hearing and ask "is THAT what you mean?"

### Step-Specific Rules:

- ONE question at a time from the prompt list
- Let the team yap freely after each prompt
- Capture key phrases verbatim
- Do NOT move to lock until real signal emerges

## CONTEXT BOUNDARIES:

- Input: Locked concept seed, team discussion
- Output: One locked sentence describing how the reference point works
- Dependencies: Step 1 complete

## MANDATORY SEQUENCE

### 1. Frame the Round

"**Round 1: The Reference Point**

This is the keystone. Everything else builds on it.

The tool shows you how far your thinking diverges from the average. But divergence from WHAT exactly?

A few angles to explore:"

### 2. Facilitate Yap (Use Prompts as Needed)

Present prompts ONE AT A TIME. Wait for team yap after each.

**Prompt A:** "Is the reference point a PROBLEM you're solving? Like 'how do people get rides?' — and the tool shows how different your solution path is from the cluster of obvious solutions?"

**Prompt B:** "Is it a QUESTION you're responding to? Like a design brief, a prompt, a challenge — and the tool shows your response's distance from the average response?"

**Prompt C:** "Is it more ambient — your general thinking patterns over time compared to the aggregate? Less about a specific problem, more about YOUR mind's signature?"

**Prompt D:** "Does the user SET the reference point, or does the tool detect it? Like — do you say 'here's what I'm working on' or does it just know?"

**Prompt E:** "Could the reference point BE the AI's own answer? You ask the same question, AI gives the vanilla response, and the tool shows how far YOUR answer arcs from that?"

### Divergent Seeds (drop these if conversation stalls)

These are lateral provocations — not answers, just sparks:

- "What if the reference point shifts depending on WHO you're diverging from? Your industry? Your generation? Your own past self?"
- "What if there IS no fixed reference point — what if the tool shows you the entire landscape of all possible responses and where yours sits in the field?"
- "Forget problems and questions. What if the reference point is a FEELING — the tool senses when your emotional state diverges from your behavioral pattern?"
- "What if the reference point is the version of you that AI would predict? The tool shows the gap between who AI thinks you are and who you actually are in this moment."
- "What if the reference point is collective — not 'divergent from AI' but 'divergent from everyone who faced this same choice this week'?"

### 3. Extract Signal

As the team yaps, listen for:
- Which framing gets the most energy
- Which one they keep coming back to
- Which one Teri's design instinct gravitates toward
- Which one creates the most vivid visualization

Reflect back: "I'm hearing [X]. Is that right?"

### 4. Anti-Pattern Check

Before locking, verify:
- Does this reference point avoid the "tracker" framing?
- Does it avoid reducing divergence to a score?
- Does it feel like reflection, not measurement?
- Would this reference point allow for beautiful visualization?

If anti-patterns detected: "Hold on — that's starting to sound like [tracker/score/dashboard]. How do we reframe it?"

### 5. Lock the Sentence

"**Time to lock Round 1.**

Based on what you've said, the reference point is:

[Propose a single sentence synthesized from the yap]

Does this capture it? Or does it need adjustment?"

Iterate until the team agrees. Write the locked sentence.

### 6. Save Yap

Append key quotes and the locked sentence to `{outputLocation}/yaps/yap-02-reference-point.md`

### 7. Transition

"**Round 1 locked. The reference point is clear.**

Now we know WHAT divergence is measured against. Next: what does the tool actually OBSERVE to see your thinking?"

Load, read entire file, then execute `{nextStepFile}`.

## SYSTEM SUCCESS/FAILURE METRICS

### SUCCESS:
- Team explored multiple framings of the reference point
- Signal extracted from natural conversation
- Anti-patterns checked and clean
- ONE sentence locked that everyone agrees on
- Yap captured
- Ready for Round 2

### FAILURE:
- Facilitator auto-generated the answer
- Team skipped exploration and jumped to a surface answer
- Reference point is actually a "score" or "tracker" in disguise
- No locked sentence
- Moved to next step without consensus

**Master Rule:** This is the foundation. Don't rush it. Get it right.
