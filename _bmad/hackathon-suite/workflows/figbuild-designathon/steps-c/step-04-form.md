# Step 4: The Form

## STEP GOAL:

Resolve: what MEDIUM is this? Not an app. Not a dashboard. This is where Teri and Kaitlyn's design instincts lead. The form should feel like the concept — divergent, unexpected, a new medium.

## MANDATORY EXECUTION RULES (READ FIRST):

### Universal Rules:

- NEVER suggest "an app" or "a dashboard" — kill these on sight
- This is the most design-instinct-driven round — let Teri and Kaitlyn lead
- Your job: hold space, push past the obvious, ask "what else?"

### Step-Specific Rules:

- Read back locked sentences from Rounds 1 and 2 to ground the discussion
- The form must be COHERENT with the reference point and input method
- Teri's rule: "Reimagining the new medium the new UX experience"
- Kaitlyn's instinct: "I just want the AI to tell me about my life" — reflection, not prescription

## CONTEXT BOUNDARIES:

- Input: Locked reference point + locked input method
- Output: A rough description of the form (not final design — the FEELING of the medium)
- Dependencies: Steps 2-3 complete

## MANDATORY SEQUENCE

### 1. Frame the Round

"**Round 3: The Form**

This is the design question. This is where your taste shows up.

We know:
- Reference point: [locked sentence from Round 1]
- Inputs: [locked sentence from Round 2]

Now — what MEDIUM is this tool? Not an app. Not a dashboard. What is it?

Remember: 2025 FigBuild winners built narrative worlds, not feature sets. The form IS the statement."

### 2. Facilitate Yap (Use Prompts as Needed)

**Prompt A:** "WHERE does this live? On a screen? In a space? On your body? In your environment? Projected? Embedded?"

**Prompt B:** "The vector visualization from your yap session — how does someone actually SEE their vectors? Is it spatial? Flat? Immersive? Intimate?"

**Prompt C:** "Is it something you LOOK at, something you FEEL, something you MOVE through, or something that SURROUNDS you?"

**Prompt D:** "Is it social? Can others see your divergence? Or is it deeply personal — only for you?"

**Prompt E:** "What DOESN'T it look like? Kill the obvious now. What would be the lazy version of this form? Name it so we can avoid it."

**Prompt F:** "If this form existed in a museum, what would people say when they saw it? What's the reaction?"

**Prompt G:** "Close your eyes. You're using this tool for the first time. What do you see? What are your hands doing? Where are you?"

### Divergent Seeds (drop these if conversation stalls)

These are wild. Most should be killed. But one might unlock something:

- "What if it's a ROOM? You walk into a space and the walls are your vector field. Your divergence is architectural — you're literally standing inside your own thinking."
- "What if it's a MIRROR that doesn't show your face — it shows the shape of your last idea? You look at yourself and see your thought pattern instead of your reflection."
- "What if it's a CONVERSATION PARTNER that only speaks in your own past divergent moves? It doesn't give advice — it reminds you of the times you went left when everyone went right."
- "What if it's a MATERIAL — like clay or ink — that responds to the divergence in what you're making? The more divergent your work, the more the material transforms."
- "What if it's a GARDEN? Your divergent thoughts grow like plants. The average ones are grass. The 1-in-10,000 moves are rare flowers you can see from across the field."
- "What if it's a SHADOW? Always with you but only visible under certain light. When you're being vanilla, no shadow. When you diverge, it stretches."
- "What if the form itself is divergent — it changes every time you use it? No two sessions look the same because the medium adapts to your thinking."
- "What if it's NOTHING visible at all? What if it's a feeling — haptic, thermal, spatial — that you sense but can't point to? A sixth sense for your own divergence."

### 3. Extract Signal

Listen for:
- What gets Teri excited (this is HER domain)
- What Kaitlyn can visualize and illustrate
- What creates the strongest emotional response
- What feels genuinely NEW — not a remix of existing mediums
- What connects the vector visualization to a lived experience

### 4. Anti-Pattern Check

Kill on sight:
- "It's like an app but..." — no. What is it on its own terms?
- "A dashboard that shows..." — dead.
- "A wearable that tracks..." — dead.
- Anything that looks like existing quantified-self products

Push for:
- What medium would make a Figma CPO say "this is why design matters"?
- What would make judges at a DESIGN competition lean forward?

### 5. Lock the Description

"**Time to lock Round 3.**

The form is:

[Propose a 1-2 sentence description of the medium and feeling]

Not the final design — the FEELING of it. Does this capture what you see?"

Iterate until agreed.

## SYSTEM SUCCESS/FAILURE METRICS

### SUCCESS:
- Form feels genuinely new — not a remix
- Coherent with reference point and inputs
- Teri and Kaitlyn energized by it
- Passes anti-pattern check (not app, not dashboard, not wearable tracker)
- Description captures the FEELING, not just the technology
- Could win a design competition on form alone

### FAILURE:
- Defaulted to "an app" or "a dashboard"
- Feels like existing products
- Team couldn't get past familiar mediums
- Form disconnected from concept

**Master Rule:** The form IS the concept made physical. If the form isn't divergent, the project isn't either.
